## 2. Break-and-retest spec

**Core definition:**
- "The break and retest trading setup is when price breaks a certain level of support or resistance, then retest that level." (bonus_1460330, 00:02:55)
- "Wait for momentum and trend to develop. Look for a stock to break a key level. Once broken, look for a retrace. On retrace, look for a hammer candle. Once PA is confirmed, enter looking for high of day." (bonus_1460330, 00:12:40)

**Displacement:**
- "All displacement is the move from where you break out." (bonus_1461019, 00:44:51)
- "Displacement only occurs when you have a candle that does not touch your previous resistance turn support." (bonus_1461019, 00:47:17)
- "You can see it much more clear on the second move. The second move is displacement." (bonus_1461019, 00:46:35)

**Stop:**
- "Stop loss = the impulsive candle that broke out. Break below that candle." (bonus_1461019, 00:07:24)
- "Risk level is always break of key level or break of trend/market structure." (bonus_1460330, 00:20:40)
- "Stop losses are not a punishment. They exist to save you from bigger losses." (coaching 2740249, 00:27:53)

**Entry candle:**
- "I like hammer candles when going long. Inverted hammer or shooting star when going short." (bonus_1460330, 00:56:13)
- "Always wait for the candle to close before entry or exit." (bonus_1460330, 00:19:47)
- "The best setups are obvious. No convincing needed. If you're having second thoughts, skip it." (coaching 2740277, 00:19:57)

**Entry procedure:**
- "Always enter with limit 10 cents above." (bonus_1460330, 01:11:55)
- "Sell into strength on option contracts." (bonus_1460330, 01:13:42)

**Invalidation:**
- "If there's no high of day or low of day continuation, most likely it's going to be choppy." (bonus_1461019, 00:43:52)
- "Break back into consolidation — this proves no momentum. Stop out." (bonus_1461019, 00:06:15)
- "2R must be achievable within the stock's average daily range. If target is $15 but stock moves $2/day — skip." (bonus_1461019, 00:15:45)

**Work-right-away principle:**
- "This is a trade you want to work right away. If the stock doesn't work right away, most likely it's going to fail." (bonus_1461019, 01:10:34)
- "Patience of 3 hours to make money in 13 minutes." (bonus_1461019, 00:32:25)

## 5. Key levels hierarchy

**Drawing methodology (bonus_1460330):**
- Start daily → 4H → 1H → 1-min (previous day only)
- 5-6 levels max, 4-7 at most
- Daily refresh: delete all levels each day
- Premarket H/L = 4:00-9:29 AM ET
- VWAP: directional bias only (above = calls, below = puts), never standalone stop

**Neto's daily levels (coaching 2740277):**

## Ambiguities

1. **"One candle rule" vs "1 kind of rule"** — used interchangeably. It is J-Dub's personal term for an order-block-based B&R where entry is triggered by one candle close. Core community members (Tony, Jack) use it freely. Neto and many students limit it to order-block-at-key-level only, or skip entirely. There is no standardized written spec for the exact entry criteria.

2. **Scaling percentages vary by market regime** — trending vs choppy produce different scaling (25% vs 50%+). No single mechanical rule.

3. **Stop-to-BE disagreement persists** — Scarface says never until structure change. Others say after TP1. No resolution in coaching files.

4. **84% YouTube video** referenced but not analyzed. The 84% figure is stated as "theoretical."

5. **Max trades/day** varies: one trader says 2, another says 1, some say 3. No hard community-wide rule.

6. **VWAP contradiction** — described as "aesthetic only" then immediately used for directional bias. Coach calls it "just an indicator, you don't really need it" (bonus_1460330, 00:30:44).

7. **Bias vs thesis** — "Don't go into the day with bias" contradicts "I make my thesis on 4H/1H daily." Unresolved tension between planning and flexibility.
