## mastermind-1-0_Lesson_5_How_To_Grow_A_Trading_Account_How_To_Spot_Trade_Rel

- "Size down in the beginning of your trading career because when you're taking losses and don't understand your system, a big loss like $450,000 will take you a long time to recover from psychologically, but if you only lose $1,000 total when learning, it accelerates your growth as a consistent trader" ([203s-244s])

- "You cannot have your account have big losses; that's the number one thing you need to manage your risk for" ([763s-776s])

- "Most traders plateau and quit during the pivoting phase after the initial growth, when they haven't yet understood that trading requires discipline and attentive market observation" ([265s-304s])

- "At the plateau stage, assess your performance by closely reviewing trading results and journaling each night to fix mistakes" ([889s-912s])

- "At the plateau stage, implement one strategy you'll trade for the foreseeable future and back test it to understand its weaknesses and strengths" ([944s-979s])

- "As a consistent trader understanding the technicals and market structure, the psychology aspect is what still holds you back and prevents that jump to profitability" ([1013s-1054s])

- "When consistent, hide your P&L while in trades because seeing +50, -50, +100, -100 fluctuations messes with your psychology and prevents unbiased chart analysis" ([1176s-1222s])

- "When consistent, don't deviate from your system, don't change strategies, don't increase size too quickly, and keep doing what got you to consistency" ([1227s-1263s])

- "If you have a $100,000 job, don't target $100,000 per month trading immediately; instead slowly supplement income starting with $20,000 per year, then $30,000, then $50,000" ([1304s-1349s])

- "Only quit your job when you make the same amount trading as your job salary and have 2-3 years of expenses saved up" ([1349s-1389s])

- "For sizing when consistent, if trading 1-2 contracts, increase by one contract every 3-4 weeks" ([1442s-1475s])

- "Don't make a big jump in contracts (like 25 to 50); a 100% jump caused loss of psychology and plateau even though win rate stayed the same" ([1465s-1622s])

- "Size back down if psychology is affected (e.g., losing $700-800 per trade when used to $700-800 good days), then slowly size back up" ([1625s-1645s])

- "Don't trade every single day; confirmation bias about Qs strength should help you too; seeing experienced traders say it's the hardest market in 10 years and sizing down validates your own discipline" ([1826s-1889s])

- "Relative strength: when Qs breaks above key level (30886) and stock consolidates underneath flag pattern near retest level, this shows relative strength for momentum" ([1968s-2064s])

- "Use Qs as correlation for conviction: if Qs is making new high of day and stock is not, stock has relative weakness; watch for breakout timing together" ([2040s-2098s])

- "When Qs is consolidating at view-up and making higher low, and stock is retesting after new highs, this is a high-probability trade because Qs has room to high of day" ([3257s-3279s])

## mastermind-1-0_Lesson_6_Part_2

- **"The 84% rule: if you have an entry criteria like a break and retest (stock breaks high of day, comes back, retests key level) and you break market structure, but later in the day (5, 10, 15, 20 minutes) the same trade sets up again with same criteria, 84% of the time this second trade should work out"** ([21s-140s])

- "After stopping out of a trade, you can reassess and reenter if the 84% rule setup occurs again, which helps psychologically overcome fear of stopped out trades" ([140s-152s])

- "Risk-to-reward of 1.2:1 is acceptable (like 1-to-2 minimum is ideal)" ([509s-535s])

- "When managing risk in trades, if stop hits a small candle, you can move stop lower to previous pivot structure because small wick shows less conviction from sellers" ([560s-599s])

- "If stock comes back near stop after breaking it, you can exit there to take the small loss rather than letting it run further" ([369s-385s])

- "Never move your stop loss just because price bounced; move it only for new market structure, not for emotion" ([143s-207s])

- "Wait for candle close to avoid whipsaws; a candle that breaks stop and later closes strong is a signal to stay in, not exit on wick" ([2455s-2508s])

- "Don't force trades; if Spy doesn't have a real retest (just consolidating at level without break and close above), don't take the trade" ([2508s-2609s])

- "Volume dies down after 11:00 AM, so trade until 11:00 AM when most volume occurs; biggest moves happen in first 1-2 hours of market open" ([2630s-2663s])

- "Focus on one thing at a time as new trader: charts (trend structure, market structure, candlesticks), then patterns, then options mechanics, then psychology, then one setup, then sizing, then job balance" ([920s-996s])

- "Don't trade indicators; trade price action, but know what indicators are" ([1057s-1065s])

- "Back test setups for at least 4 months minimum on a daily basis to know if it's profitable and matches your personality" ([1620s-1660s])

- "Start with 1-2 contracts when learning; this is emphasized multiple times because traders who don't listen blow up $5,000 in a day" ([1429s-1734s])

- "Don't quit your job until 1 year of consistent trading + 2-3 years of expenses backed up" ([1938s-1974s])

- "Trade the first hour when busy with job/family because first hour has most volume and ballroom for volatility" ([1974s-2005s])

- "If can't trade first hour, trade the 5-minute timeframe instead of 1-minute later in the day to get the same break and retest setup" ([2088s-2109s])

- "Place stop loss at candle body or wick depending on confluence: if body has more confluence use body, if equal wicks use wick low, if small wick use body" ([2113s-2133s])

- "Only enter on hammer or inverted hammer or shooting star candles for best trades, but really look for bearish/bullish price action context" ([1014s-1062s])

- "Never use limit orders before market open; wait for market to open, see price, then decide entry speed" ([2060s-2081s])

## mastermind-1-0_Lesson_7_How_To_Play_The_Open_Multi_Time_Frame_Analysis

- "Use Heikin-Ashi (HA) candles on 1-hour and 4-hour timeframes to identify higher timeframe patterns by filtering out noise" ([88s-211s])

- "Playing the open strategy: much more emphasis on key levels than break and retest; don't have to wait for retest per se; need to act fast because trading price action and higher timeframe" ([221s-250s])

- "Playing the open: you still wait for candle close before entering, but second that candle closes, you need to enter immediately because momentum is ongoing" ([259s-273s])

- "Playing the open: you don't need a retest of level or pattern, but you need to understand hold of level or pattern" ([289s-308s])

- "Example: if bare flag pattern exists intraday, enter on inverted hammer candle off that bare flag and ride to low of day" ([311s-331s])

- "Break and retest: enter after trend is clear intraday; needs higher low formed or lower high or key level break. Playing the open: enter before clear intraday trend because you have clear trend on higher timeframe" ([346s-413s])

- "For playing the open, risk level is break of another key level or higher timeframe pattern, not just break of market structure" ([418s-435s])

- "Playing the open: give your trade room to breathe; don't panic after entering; expect first 5 minutes volatility; your risk level must account for this" ([508s-584s])

- "Profit targets for playing the open: high of day, low of day, key levels, pattern higher/lower lows" ([611s-635s])

- "Edge for playing the open comes from higher timeframe; you need market context and ensure market aligns with your thesis (don't force if overall market is bullish but your thesis is bearish on one stock)" ([640s-731s])

- "Can also use playing the open strategy later in day, not just first 5 minutes, once market context validates the higher timeframe thesis" ([590s-607s])

- "Example setup: bare flag on 1-hour HA on Spy, pre-market level marked, break below pre-market low, retest of pre-market low level, aggressive entry on opening dip, profit targets at previous day structures (e.g., 420.74 cycle level, 420 key level)" ([1007s-1107s])

- "Example: wedge pattern on 1-hour HA on Tesla showing downtrend, wait for break of wedge pattern, pre-market low and high mark intraday key levels, break below triggers short, inverted hammer confirms retest entry, profit targets from 1-hour pattern" ([1263s-1536s])

- "Scale profits at key levels and cycles to hold to full profit target and manage volatility" ([1156s-1217s])

## mastermind-1-0_Lesson_8_A_Setups_How_To_Create_Discipline

- "Discipline defined: practice of training yourself to follow rules and conduct yourself with behavior defined within your rules, with penalties for non-compliance" ([28s-45s])

- "Three types of discipline: (1) Preventive - establish expectations, guidelines, behavior (rules). (2) Supportive - occurs after mistake with warning (yellow card). (3) Corrective - occurs after failing supportive discipline, has consequences" ([276s-441s])

- "Preventive discipline example: just journaling doesn't work; need supportive discipline (warning) and corrective discipline (consequence like running 5km) to change behavior" ([311s-588s])

- "To create discipline: (1) Write down where you want to be in a year (realistic goal). (2) Understand your strengths. (3) Understand your weaknesses. (4) Focus on fixing weaknesses, not perfecting strengths. (5) Start with small size" ([601s-914s])

- "Example: if 200→170 pounds goal, and gym is strength but diet is weakness, focus on fixing diet while maintaining gym; fixing weakness increases overall probability of reaching goal" ([649s-817s])

- "Don't go all-in on new discipline plan (like gym every day for week); you'll fail after 8 days. Start small (3-4 days/week) with rest days to build sustainable consistency" ([834s-988s])

- "Discipline in trading: making rules and following them is what separates traders from gamblers" ([1013s-1118s])

- "Only trade 1-2 contracts when learning; this is critical" ([1429s-1734s])

- "If you understand setup but scared to take it, that's a psychology issue, not a setup issue; you must execute" ([1213s-1228s])
