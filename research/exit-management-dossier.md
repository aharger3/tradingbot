## COMPRESSED (for quick reference)

### Scaling by regime
| Market | HOD scale | Rest | Source |
|--------|-----------|------|--------|
| Trending | 15-25% | Hold 75-85% to HTF target | Mastermind, Coaching, YouTube |
| Choppy | 50-100% (scalp) | Nothing left | Mastermind |

### Target hierarchy
HOD/LOD (always) → Opening candle → PDH/PDL → PMH/PML → Psych numbers → Old highs/lows → Gap fill → ATH → HTF pattern targets → Weekly pivots

### Stop split (ATTRIBUTE EVERY QUOTE)
- **Tony/Accelerator + J-Dub/YouTube:** Move to BE after TP1 hits, ratchet to each target. Never before TP1 hits.
- **Scarface:** NEVER move stop until market structure changes. Stop = break of impulsive candle / key level / structure. Body close rule (not wick).
- **Swing:** 30-50% option value stop.
- **Scalp options:** 10-15% contract loss.

### Trailing methods
1. **Bar-by-bar** (runner) — move stop every other candle, exit when candle closes below prev candle low
2. **OB-to-OB** — trail from one order block's wick to next
3. **Option value %** — 5-10% drop normal, 20% in hot market (mental stop)
4. **Ratchet** — move stop to each hit target

## HEADLINE FINDINGS

1. **NO single scaling rule.** Regime decides: trending = light HOD scale (15-25%), hold majority; choppy = take 50-100% at HOD, scalp it. J-Dub: 15-20% HOD, 20% intermediate, 50% final target, 10% runner. Tony: ~80% at first target. All context-driven.

2. **HOD/LOD is NEVER skipped.** Universal rule across all 4 sources: always take 1 scale at HOD/LOD regardless of R:R. Even 1:1. Only exception: 84% rule re-entry where continuation probability is higher.

3. **BE-after-TP1 vs never-move-stop = real instructor split.** Accelerator + YouTube: move stop to BE after TP1 hits, then ratchet to each target. Mastermind (Scarface): NEVER move stop until market structure changes. This is Scarface vs Tony/J-Dub, not settled.

4. **Bar-by-bar trailing = standard runner method.** Move stop up every 1-2 candles after TP1, stop out when candle closes below previous candle's low. Also: OB-to-OB (trail from one order block to next).

5. **Option-specific trailing:** 5-10% drop in option value as trailing stop; 20% trailing in hot market. Mental stops for options (worry about underlying, not contract).

### ACCELERATOR / YOUTUBE POSITION: Move to BE after TP1

**Accelerator:**
> "Move stop to BE only AFTER first profit target hits — you can't move your stop to break even if it doesn't hit first profit target, let the trade breathe." (1450449 [00:54:17])
> "After first scale: can move stop to breakeven (1:1), then hold runner." (1450449 [00:53:51-54:42], [00:45:16])
> "Then trail stop to each hit target." (1450449 [00:53:51-54:42])

**YouTube:**
> "Once it hits your first profit target, that's when you can move your stop underline to break even if you're trading options, bam, underline to break even." (9sk9oJ11LfI_transcript.txt, [2975s-2980s])
> "I don't move my stop unless I have a first high of day or low of day scale." (xhD_SRbyMNE_transcript.txt, [131s-136s])

### SCARFACE POSITION: Never move stop until structure changes

**Mastermind (Scarface primary):**
> "Do NOT move stop until market structure changes. If you move before, you're taking risk based on your own fear, not the chart." (mastermind-4-0_1461030, 00:29:33)
> "Do NOT change stop until market structure changes." (mastermind-4-0_1461037, 01:09:58)
> "Stop = break of impulsive candle / key level / market structure." (mastermind-4-0_1461030 — implied across multiple)
> "Body close rule: only exit on candle body close below stop, not wick." (mastermind-5-0_1461045, 00:06:29)
> "If you break back into consolidation — that proves no momentum. Stop out." (mastermind-5-0_1461045, 00:06:29)

### Analysis: who said what

| Instructor | Position | Source |
|-----------|----------|--------|
| Scarface (mastermind) | NEVER move stop until market structure changes | mastermind-4-0 |
| Tony (accelerator) | Move to BE after TP1 hits, ratchet to each target | 1450449 |
| J-Dub (YouTube) | Move to BE after first scale taken, bar-by-bar trail rest | Multiple YouTube |
| Coaching (psychology) | After first scale = can move to BE (1:1), hold runner | mastermind-1-0 |

**Likely reconciliation:** The "never move" rule applies to the INITIAL stop before TP1 hits. Once the trade proves itself (hits first target / scales at HOD), you CAN move stop to BE and trail. Scarface's stricter version may be for beginners to prevent premature stop-tightening out of fear — the 4-outcomes framework (big win / small win / BE / small loss) is designed to prevent the big loss, which a moved stop can inadvertently create if done wrong.

## 4. TRAILING METHODS

### Method 1: Bar-by-bar (most common)

**YouTube:**
> "A bar by bar is basically after price gets above your key level. Now what you're going to do is move your stop up to every other candle... the way you stop out on a bar by bar is when your current candle closes underneath your previous candle." (DUHq2dJePIM_transcript.txt, [4906s-4941s])

**Mastermind:**
> "J-Dub includes 10% bar-by-bar runner in his scaling model." (mastermind-5-0_1461066, 00:23:23 — 10% portion trail bar-by-bar)

**Used for:** Secondary/tertiary scales after HOD/LOD taken. Applies to runner portion (5-20% of position).

### Method 2: OB-to-OB (order block trail)

**Accelerator:**
> "Trailing stop anchor: move stop OB-to-OB as trade runs." (1450449 [01:02:10])

**Used for:** Swing trades, higher timeframe plays where multiple OBs exist on path to HTF target.

### Method 3: Option value % (mental)

**Mastermind:**
> "Trailing stop: 5-10% drop in option value, or market structure break." (mastermind-4-0_1461035, 00:40:27)
> "In hot market: 20% trailing stop." (mastermind-4-0_1461035, 00:41:34)

**Coaching-bonus:**
> "Mental stops used. Hard stops only for trailers. Beginners can use hard stops." (mastermind-4-0_1461035, 00:39:57)

**Used for:** Option traders (primary audience). Applied to contract value, not underlying. Hot market = wider tolerance (20%), normal market = 5-10%.

### Method 4: Ratchet to each hit target

**Accelerator:**
> "Move stop to each hit target as they get taken." (1450449 [00:53:51-54:42])

**Used for:** Multi-scale strategies (25/50/25, thirds). As each target level hits, stop ratchets to that level.

### Method 5: Chart alerts + mental stops

**Mastermind:**
> "Trailers use mental stops with chart alerts, not hard stops." (mastermind-4-0_1461037, 01:20:03)

**Note:** Options traders use mental stops because option pricing introduces slippage beyond underlying — hard stops on options can trigger on momentary volatility that doesn't reflect the underlying level.

### Ambiguities

1. **Scarface vs Tony/J-Dub on stop management** — never-move vs BE-after-TP1. Unresolved across all 4 sources. Likely: never-move = beginner rule to prevent fear-based tightening; BE-after-TP1 = once trade proved itself.

2. **Scaling %s are examples, not rules** — 20/60/20, 15/20/50/10, 25/50/25, thirds, 50/25/25 — all appear. No single "correct" split. Context + instructor preference.

3. **Fixed % scaling vs discretionary** — coaching-bonus flags "fixed % was hiding discomfort" (coaching 2740299), suggesting even the scaling plan should be adjusted based on the specific trade feeling, not mechanical.

4. **Runner size (5-20%)** — no rule for how much to hold. YouTube shows examples (2 contracts of many), J-Dub says 10%, mastermind example says 20%.

5. **Option % trailing vs structure-based** — 5-10% contract value drop vs market structure break. Which takes priority when they conflict? Not addressed.

6. **When does "never move stop" yield to BE?** — No explicit condition in Scarface's statements. If you never move, do you always hold full size to either target or stop? This would make BE-outcomes impossible (contradiction with 4-outcomes framework).
