## RELATIVE STRENGTH / RELATIVE WEAKNESS — ALL DEFINITIONS

### Definition 1 — Percentage Comparison (Most Objective)
> "AMD is already up 5%... Look at the QQQs. The QQQs is up 0.6%. AMD is already up 5% on the day. This is clear relative strength compared to every other ticker in the market."
> — `yk0ar-wDMXU_transcript.txt`, [1914s-2366s]

**Method:** Compare % change. If stock X% > QQQ Y% = RS. If stock negative while QQQ positive = RW.

### Definition 2 — Ratio Method
> "Let's say the QQQ is moving higher. Tesla, let's say the Qs moves up one point. Tesla moves up two points. That means if the Qs moves up two points, Tesla, just based off this relative strength, can move up four points. That's relative strength or even more points. Maybe it could be five, whereas Apple might be like a one to one. If Qs moves up one, Apple's moving up one. There's not too much relative strength there."
> — `JB61SBv4Syw_transcript.txt`, [2570s-2622s]

**Method:** Compare the ratio of stock move to QQQ move. >1:1 = RS. 1:1 = no edge.

### Definition 3 — Divergence (Opposite Direction)
> "When you say relative strength, does that mean QQQ is moving down, AMD is moving up? ... Yeah, that's relative weakness... So when they're moving in the same direction and one's moving more aggressively than another, that's relative strength and relative weakness. When it's doing the opposite, yeah, it's basically called divergence."
> — `yk0ar-wDMXU_transcript.txt`, [2298s-2304s]

**Method:** Stock making HIGHER HIGHS while QQQ goes to the DOWNSIDE = divergence/strong RS.

### Definition 4 — Visual (Side-by-Side Chart)
> "Look at the charts combine right beside each other. You'll be able to tell if there's relative strength or weakness, right? ... If it's if you're trying to decide if there's relative strength or weakness on the day you're probably looking too hard, right? ... It has to be so clear right as soon as you look at the chart. You're like oh that has relative weakness"
> — `tr7IdqVgdIg_transcript.txt`, [452s-512s]

**Method:** If you have to question whether it's RS, it's not. Must be visually obvious on a side-by-side chart.

### Definition 5 — Key Level Holding vs Breaking
> "QQQ was dropping right to its 433 82 level... But at the same time you can see Tesla was holding above 181 63s right so we were saying that this was a good low-risk entry"
> — `lvPYyI8AaSg_transcript.txt`, [12s-43s]

**Method:** QQQ drops but stock HOLDS above key support = RS. QQQ flat while stock moves down = RW.

### Definition 6 — Buyers Stepping In More Aggressively
> "We really just want to look for anything that is showing buyers stepping in more aggressively than spying cues, which basically means anything that's not consolidating as much."
> — `eP8A-vf-eWM_transcript.txt`, [2224s-2231s]

### Definition 10 — Kam's Definitive Framework
> "I try to think about it this way: when your ticker and the indices are outside ranges & moving in the same direction you have correlation. If direction / trend is aligned AND your ticker is leading the move, then you have real RS. If indices are inside range + chopping but your ticker is leading, you have RS but no correlation - that was the situation this morning with META. That's when you need to know a ticker's character to know if it can decouple / move independently, especially without a catalyst"
> — Kam, Discord `trade-feedback.json`, 2025-12-22
