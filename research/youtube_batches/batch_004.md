**1. Break-and-retest:**
- The break-and-retest is the core setup, requiring a clear break of a key level (previous day high/low, opening range, order block) followed by a retest of that level as support/resistance before continuation. "break and retest, we're going to be looking for continuations lower down towards these, this double bottom." (9sk9oJ11LfI_transcript.txt, [339s])
- A "gap and go" to the downside is a specific variant where price gaps below a level, retests, and continues lower. "This is what's called the gap and go, right? But this is towards the downside." (9sk9oJ11LfI_transcript.txt, [2057s-2060s])
- The retest must show "displacement" (strong momentum) away from the level before the pullback. "you'd rather wait for the break above and then the retest and then push back towards the highs" (9te7G9JT1rw_transcript.txt, [3293s])
- Immediate consolidation after a break is NOT a valid retest. "I wouldn't really consider this a retest though. It is trying to hold this 429 24 level. This is much more immediate consolidation in my opinion." (9te7G9JT1rw_transcript.txt, [2585s-2594s])
- The best entries are as close to the risk level (stop loss) as possible. "if you are looking for a similar entry, right, you always want to buy as close to that risk level as possible" (AjdBNs8qY7c_transcript.txt, [1938s])
- A trade that works out within the first five minutes is a good sign for continuation; if it chops, it's a bad sign. "if a trade works out... majority of my trades if you've seen they usually work out right away... Right within the first right, I'd say five minutes if a trade starts working out within the five minutes There's usually a good sign that's going to have continuation higher" (AjdBNs8qY7c_transcript.txt, [1901s-1922s])
- The break-and-retest is the foundation of all trades. "all of our trades are primarily based off of the retest" (AjdBNs8qY7c_transcript.txt, [3771s])
