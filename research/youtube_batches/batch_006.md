**1. Break-and-retest:**
- The break-and-retest works best in trending markets, not range-bound markets. "the best break and retest best happens when you're when you're trading in a trend right when you're trading when you're trading in range range markets right what happens when you try and trade the breaking retest is that this will end up just failing and reversing back towards the downside" (HJ5F0avmOzk_transcript.txt, [2723s-2738s])
- Displacement (impulsive break) is required for a valid setup, especially on lower timeframes. "We need to see that buyers are indeed stepping out and that's the way that we would want to take a trade" (HKn_S8wtKr0_transcript.txt, [4042s-4046s])
- The amount of displacement needed is context-dependent: higher timeframe moves require less displacement on the entry timeframe. "When you're looking at a displacement, it depends on the move, right? So if it's, for example, if it's a higher timeframe move, right? And you know that the higher timeframe looks really good. Sometimes you can, on the lower timeframe, you don't really even need that much, that big of a displacement, just because you're trying to enter as fast as possible because you're looking for that higher timeframe move" (HJm2qQ673co_transcript.txt, [3750s-3793s])
- The short side often offers only one entry before dumping, while the long side offers multiple entries. "A lot of times for the short side, it usually offers you one entry and then just kind of dumps, right? Whereas the long side, that's why we like it so much is because sometimes it offers you a few entries, you can wait for confirmation in this case, right?" (H8L_aspf3u0_transcript.txt, [3586s-3594s])
- Immediate retests (aggressive) vs. classic retests (with displacement) are both valid entry models. "This one's more aggressive on the one minute, basically an immediate retest. This is like a more, I wouldn't say advanced, but this is like a more aggressive entry model." (H8L_aspf3u0_transcript.txt, [2370s-2377s])
- The previous day high is a stronger level than a 5-minute opening range. "the previous day high is always going to be a stronger level than a 5minute opening range uh but if you don't have the previous day higher it's not any around that then you could be looking for the 5 minute opening range" (HJ5F0avmOzk_transcript.txt, [2001s-2011s])
- If you miss the initial move, there is usually only one more good move after the immediate retest. "if you miss usually the initial kind of move right there's usually maybe one more good move after that if you catch like the immediate retest but after that it's going to to be just kind of back inside this range" (HJ5F0avmOzk_transcript.txt, [4307s-4319s])
- A break-and-retest requires continuation; if you get a reversal instead, the setup fails. "what we need for the breaking retest is continuation but instead of continuation we get a reversal" (HJ5F0avmOzk_transcript.txt, [2743s-2748s])

**3. 84% rule / re-entries:**
