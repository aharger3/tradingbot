**1. Break-and-retest:**
- The break-and-retest is the core setup, often combined with multiple confluences like higher timeframe structure, order blocks, and key levels for high-probability entries. "Number one higher time frame flat top breaking out of flat top. That's number one number two Strong price action coming back into a key level number three High of day retest, which was the first setup that we were looking for high of day retest" (7DM97TbfgNo_transcript.txt, [132s-148s])
- A "classic" break-and-retest involves a strong impulsive move, a clear break of a level, and a retest that holds, leading to continuation. "Classic break and retest, right? It gets above it. Now we're going to be essentially using this area as support." (7l8yqcapJ2c_transcript.txt, [249s])
- The retest can be of various levels: previous day high, pre-market high, five-minute high, or an order block. "All I was looking for was a break above and then the retest of Tesla" (7_RtCugLJfs_transcript.txt, [65s])
- A key nuance is that the best retests often coincide with order blocks, and the entry can be a limit order at the level, waiting for a reaction, or waiting for confirmation (e.g., a strong candle closure). "the retest of this key area on the higher time frames... go on to let's say the lower time frames and look for any sort of entry confirmation candles, right? Either confirmation, right? You can put a limit order but the majority of times you want to wait for confirmation" (7kajZjCStT8_transcript.txt, [878s])
- A failed retest (e.g., a weak candle closure above a level) is a signal to avoid the trade. "I said I wanted a five strong candle closure above the pre pre pre market highs, right? and you can see that We'd even have a five minute candle closure above that level and it was very weak as well Right, so you can't you can't look for lungs it once you see a five minute candle closure like that" (81-nekY3l0U_transcript.txt, [1388s-1407s])
- A retest after the main objective has been hit is considered less optimal. "this retest is not as good as the original retest which would have been off your previous high day because now we already hit your main objective" (7auleEXxAz8_transcript.txt, [5013s])
- **Pattern:** Across transcripts, the break-and-retest is consistently described as the primary entry model, with a strong emphasis on waiting for confirmation (candle closure, price action reaction) rather than anticipating the break.

**2. "One candle rule" / opening candle retest:**
- The opening candle (often the first 5-minute candle) acts as a key level for the day. A break and retest of this candle is a high-probability setup. "Breaks above its opening candle print Which is also usually this pre-market high and then you want to look for a retest of the opening candle print for a quick Move towards the upside" (81-nekY3l0U_transcript.txt, [327s-334s])
- The "one candle rule" is often used in conjunction with other levels like the five-minute high or an order block. "we wanted to come into the five minute high here. You wanted to come into the five minute high plus this one candle rule and you want to see buyers step in for of course and move back up to high of day." (7auleEXxAz8_transcript.txt, [910s])
- A candle closure below the "one candle rule" level can act as a stop-loss trigger. "candle closure below the one candle ruler that last level defense of one sixty three thirty twos" (7auleEXxAz8_transcript.txt, [3046s])
- The concept can be extended to higher timeframes, like the first candle of the year or month. "And it's funny about 2024 as well. (7l8yqcapJ2c_transcript.txt, [708s])
- **Pattern:** The "one candle rule" is a specific application of the break-and-retest, focusing on the first candle of a given timeframe. It is frequently mentioned as a key level to watch for both entries and exits.

**3. 84% rule / re-entries:**
- The 84% rule is a re-entry strategy used when the first trade thesis is invalidated (i.e., the stop-loss is hit). It is not a re-entry for a trade that was prematurely cut. "the 84% rule is only valid if the first trade would have stopped you out" (7kajZjCStT8_transcript.txt, [1351s])
- It is often used when price reclaims a key level after a false breakdown. "84% rule majority of the times, right? I'll always look to get in on the reclaim because it doesn't always come back down to retest" (7kajZjCStT8_transcript.txt, [3771s])
- The 84% rule can be used with increased size if the setup is very clear. "On 84%, usually, if I enter in a size on the 84%, I usually, I kind of put, if it's a clear as this one, right? I'll put in like, I don't put in double the risk, but I put in a little bit more." (7l8yqcapJ2c_transcript.txt, [5713s])
- A common application is after a break-and-retest fails, and price comes back to retest the same level again. "And then you can see where we have that nice push and that's exactly where we had the 84% roll coming to effect, same stop as before, same targets as before, was that continuation back towards the highs." (7l8yqcapJ2c_transcript.txt, [5484s])
- **Contradiction/Clarification:** One trader notes that the 84% rule is invalidated if the original thesis was never broken, meaning a premature exit does not qualify for a re-entry. "the 84% rule is invalidated only if it doesn't break your thesis the first time. So in this case right my thesis was actually still intact on the first trade so I never should have stopped at to begin with so that's why if you entered on the 84% rule doesn't really count because it never broke structure to begin with" (7kajZjCStT8_transcript.txt, [1298s])

**4. Order blocks:**
- An order block is an area where large market participants have placed significant orders, often identified by a down-close candle in an uptrend or an up-close candle in a downtrend. "So what is an order block? So an order block is an area where large market participants have placed significant buy or sell orders." (7l8yqcapJ2c_transcript.txt, [106s])
- The highest probability entry within an order block is from the top of the wick to the body of the candle (for a buy in an uptrend). "But you'll see the best and highest probability order blocks will occur when priced at the top of the wick to the body, right? The top of the wick to the body is the best area to look for a buy opportunity when we're in an uptrend." (7l8yqcapJ2c_transcript.txt, [298s])
- Order blocks are most effective when they have multiple confluences, such as displacement (a fair value gap) or alignment with a key level. "A valid order block in the highest probability order blocks occur when there's multiple confluences." (7l8yqcapJ2c_transcript.txt, [3013s])
- Order blocks are invalidated if price closes back above (for a bearish block) or below (for a bullish block) the block. "So the order block is only invalid until we break back above it." (7l8yqcapJ2c_transcript.txt, [824s])
- Higher timeframe order blocks are more reliable than intraday ones. "What timeframe would you say that the order blocks would get respected the most? Higher timeframe, of course, higher timeframe order blocks, the higher timeframe levels are always gonna respect it more than intraday" (7l8yqcapJ2c_transcript.txt, [1369s])
- Order blocks are less reliable in choppy, ranging markets. "How do order blocks work in this market? That has been very choppy. They usually, they don't work. You'll see, whenever order blocks are in a chopping market, they'll be invalidated." (7l8yqcapJ2c_transcript.txt, [4447s])
- **Pattern:** Order blocks are a foundational concept, often used as the precise entry zone within a broader break-and-retest setup. The focus is on identifying "down close candles" in uptrends and "up close candles" in downtrends.

- **Pattern:** Key levels are the foundation for all trade setups. The most frequently cited and trusted levels are previous day highs/lows, followed by intraday levels like high/low of day and opening range.

**7. Exits:**

- **Pattern:** The long and short playbooks are mirror images. The same technical tools are applied, but the direction of the trade is determined by the higher timeframe trend and relative strength/weakness.
