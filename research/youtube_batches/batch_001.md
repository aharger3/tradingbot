**1. Break-and-retest:**

- The best entries often come from a break of a level, a strong displacement, and then a retest with a confirming candle (e.g., hammer). "the best entry on meta now would be a would be waiting until we break this range or this high of day... I like 518 31s and then retest that now" ( -_dz8WqgRB8_transcript.txt , [2818s-2822s])
- A failed retest (e.g., first candle closes below the level) can be a reason to wait for a second, cleaner retest. **4. Order blocks:**
- Order blocks are defined as up-close or down-close candles on a given timeframe. "the order blocks are just up close candles" ( -k6ayIaeOzs_transcript.txt , [5216s-5217s])

- The strength of an order block is judged by the size of the candle body; a small body is a weaker order block. "this is your up close candle this five minute, but it's not really that strong of an order block Since the body's so small" ( -_dz8WqgRB8_transcript.txt , [4119s-4122s])
- Higher timeframe order blocks (e.g., daily, weekly) are more significant and can act as major support/resistance. "Weekly order block that we're in all the way down here" ( -_dz8WqgRB8_transcript.txt , [97s-99s])
- A break below an order block invalidates the setup and is a stop-loss trigger. "I stopped out of Tesla basically right after we broke back below the order block" (0aoKhSUs-LM_transcript.txt, [135s-142s])
