**1. Break-and-retest:**
- The break-and-retest works best in a clear trend; in choppy/ranging markets it's much more difficult and prone to fakes. "the break and retest works the best in a clear trend. What happens if there's not a clear trend in the break and retest? Sometimes we'll get chopped up because we can easily get faked out" (MT5WOn4O0cw_transcript.txt, [4462s-4470s])
- A key requirement is strong displacement (impulsive candle) before the retest; without it, the setup is lower probability. "I'd like to see a little bit more displacement there on Apple before retesting." (MF84vfpsJGw_transcript.txt, [946s-950s])
- The retest level often coincides with multiple confluences (order block, opening range, previous day high/low) for higher probability. "So you can see price reclaim is key area nice retest office area this where I get him on This candle confirmation stop just a break below looking for high day and looking for continuation above" (MU2FECBI6jQ_transcript.txt, [1620s-1632s])
- A "reclaim" (breaking back above a level after a failed breakout) is considered a form of break-and-retest and is preferred over buying breakouts. "I will look to buy reclaims. Reclaims are some of my favorite trades. A breakout is what I consider buying something above your previous day highs or above your hive day. - The break-and-retest can be applied on higher timeframes (daily, 15-min) with the entry model on a lower timeframe. "it's essentially a breaking retest on the higher time frames your entry model doesn't doesn't really matter as much right as long as you know what's happening on the higher time frames your entry model could be breaking retest. - A clean, classic break-and-retest is preferred over choppy price action. "Whereas if you want to retest off your previous day highs, you kind of want clean price action. **2. "One candle rule" / opening candle retest:**
- The one candle rule states: in an uptrend, the last down close candle acts as support; in a downtrend, the last up close candle acts as resistance. "the one candle rule simply states is in an uptrend, the last down close candle is going to be used as support." (M-Pxkn5wjjA_transcript.txt, [97s-103s])
- The entry is on the retest of that candle, looking for a candle closure showing buyers/sellers stepping in, with the stop at the break of the one candle rule. "all I'm going to do is mark out this candle and now what we would like for the stock to do is come back, retest, show some strong price action and we can look for continuation back up into high of day." (M-Pxkn5wjjA_transcript.txt, [644s-652s])
- The opening candle print (first 5-min or 1-min candle) is a key level that is defended throughout the day. "As long as Tesla is above your opening candle print, I do kind of expect it to move higher here." (MC0fw3ThEwc_transcript.txt, [413s-416s])
- The opening range break can be used for both long and short setups, with the retest of the opening candle being the entry trigger. "So we broke above that opening range candle. - The one candle rule can align with other key levels (order blocks, previous day highs/lows) for confluence. "Tesla also coming back into this kind of key level slash one candle rule near the 315s" (MF84vfpsJGw_transcript.txt, [1640s-1646s])

**3. 84% rule / re-entries:**
- The 84% rule is a re-entry strategy after being stopped out of a trade, based on the idea that the original thesis was correct but the entry/management was wrong. "The reason the 84% rule works is because for your first trade, your entry or stop loss was wrong." (M-Pxkn5wjjA_transcript.txt, [526s-531s])
- Three steps: 1) The trade must be in your strategy, 2) Evaluate conditions (did it break trend? was stop too tight? could it still work?), 3) Re-enter using the exact same entry criteria on a reclaim. "step one, the trade has to be in your strategy." (M-Pxkn5wjjA_transcript.txt, [279s-281s]) "If those confluences are happening in your trade, then we come to step three. And this means that if the trade does come back to our entry, we can re-enter using the exact same entry criteria." (M-Pxkn5wjjA_transcript.txt, [443s-452s])
- The re-entry is typically on a candle closure above/below the original level (a reclaim). "We're waiting for a candle closure above the same level that we were trying to enter on. So, this is called a reclaim entry." (M-Pxkn5wjjA_transcript.txt, [466s-473s])
- The 84% rule is most effective on higher-probability setups with a strong thesis. "And the reason I added more contracts here is because this is a 84% rule, right? On top of that, I had a very strong thesis on Tesla because of how strong earnings was and therefore I entered into a little bit more of a position here, right?" (LyHxlS52KJg_transcript.txt, [197s-211s])
- It can be applied multiple times as long as the thesis is not invalidated. "sometimes it might take a couple tries maybe you tried shorting it here and you lost then you can just hit the the 84% roll right sometimes it happens first try and it's great sometimes it happens it takes a couple of tries but as long as it doesn't invalidate your thesis you can you can take it a couple of tries" (Mta32jhu44s_transcript.txt, [5211s-5224s])
- The 84% rule is more applicable to scalps; for higher timeframe trades, conviction is higher and re-entries are less needed. "So the 84% rule is yeah, like sometimes I'll look for the 84% rule a lot of the times right I usually look for higher probability trades so majority of the times the trades that I look to take anyways are a high probability anyways due to the higher time frames, so a lot of the times right the 84% rule really applies to more on like scalps wise" (N2XK-q0oa8I_transcript.txt, [5616s-5657s])
