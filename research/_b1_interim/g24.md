## 1. Break-and-retest (core setup)

**Rule: Break-and-retest is the fundamental strategy.**
> "for us, right, it's just a simple break in retest rate. So we're just looking to enter in right here." ([Ns], [503s-506s])

**Rule: The strategy requires a displacement move first.**
> "the fundamental strategy is simply a move up, a displacement move here." ([Ns], [530s-533s])

**Rule: The basic sequence is break, retest, then entry on the retest for a new high of day push.**
> "in the most basic terms, right, it's just the break, the retest, and you're just entering on the retest for a new high day push." ([Ns], [563s-569s])

**Rule: The setup requires a clear uptrend and a flat top (resistance).**
> "When we have a clear uptrend on this trade right here, we have a clear uptrend. And then what did we have? We had a flat top, right? So what does the flat top signify? It signifies that there is this is a resistance." ([Ns], [2054s-2065s])

**Rule: The break must be an impulsive candle with strong displacement.**
> "And if we break above this with an impulsive candle and have a strong displacement, right, it shows that buyers are buying the stock up and obviously sellers are weak above this level." ([Ns], [2065s-2074s])

**Rule: The retest entry trigger is a strong hammer stick candle close or a wick that develops underneath the level.**
> "So the price action for me, you guys know I'm either looking for a strong hammer stick candle close or I'm looking for a wick to develop right underneath." ([Ns], [2108s-2113s])

**Rule: A preferred retest signal is a wick that goes below the level and then closes above it.**
> "whenever it's a key level, I like to actually see the wick go below and then close above because that shows that sellers did try to bring it down, but buyers brought it up really aggressively." ([Ns], [2117s-2125s])

**Rule: Entry is on the second candle after the retest signal, once more strength is seen.**
> "And then once this candle closes on the second candle, I'll be looking to enter in right on the second candle once I see a little bit more strength and I enter in." ([Ns], [2126s-2132s])

**Rule: The stop loss is placed right below the break level (the key level).**
> "So therefore, the stop loss is obviously going to be right below this level." ([Ns], [2082s-2085s])

**Rule: The first profit target is always high of day (HOD).**
> "Your first profit target is always going to be high day where you scale" ([Ns], [2090s-2092s])

**Rule: The second profit target is another key level.**
> "and then your second key level or whatever, right?" ([Ns], [2093s-2095s])

**Rule: A level is a zone, not a specific tick.**
> "every level is a zone, right? You have to consider the levels like a zone. Like it's not like the trade is going to come down to your level specifically, right?... It can be like anywhere from like a two to ten cents zone." ([Ns], [2392s-2424s])

**Rule: A trade that follows the plan is a "good trade," regardless of the outcome.**
> "The trade's result of winning and losing does not define a good trade. What defines a good trade is your pre-plan." ([Ns], [2190s-2194s])

## 7. Exits

**Rule: The first profit target is always high of day (HOD) where you scale.**
> "Your first profit target is always going to be high day where you scale" ([Ns], [2090s-2092s])

**Rule: The second profit target is another key level.**
> "and then your second key level or whatever, right?" ([Ns], [2093s-2095s])

**Rule: In a trending market, take 20% off at HOD and hold the majority of the position.**
> "But then after that, when you make that new high of day, you're going to obviously only take, let's say 20% off here, and then you're going to hold the majority of your position because you're in a trending market." ([Ns], [633s-642s])

**Rule: In a market with no continuation (reverses at HOD), take 100% off at HOD (scalp the strategy).**
> "if we enter in at this level, at the retest level, and it does move up to high of day, but it reverses that high of day, we're still taking the high of day scale. But at this point, right, rather than taking, let's say 20 to 40% off a high of day, now we're taking 100% off and we're just scalping the strategy rather than holding the trailers." ([Ns], [597s-612s])

## 9. Trade selection

**Rule: The system should be simple, with few confluences.**
> "the more simple you can make your system, the more simple the entry and exit criteria is, the less confluences you need, the better it will be long term" ([Ns], [471s-478s])

**Rule: QQQ alignment is a required confluence.**
> "You need QQQ. You need to understand, you know, how much displacement this has." ([Ns], [554s-557s])
> "you simply look over at QQQ if QQQ is also aligning. It's simple. You take the trade." ([Ns], [1803s-1806s])

**Rule: A good trade is defined by following the plan, not by the outcome.**
> "The trade's result of winning and losing does not define a good trade. What defines a good trade is your pre-plan." ([Ns], [2190s-2194s])

**Rule: In a consolidation market, decrease your size.**
> "We're in a consolidation market and therefore the win rate does decrease and therefore let me just decrease my size in this specific market." ([Ns], [696s-701s])

**Rule: In a trending market, increase your size back up.**
> "But then when the market starts trending again, I'm going to increase my size back up to wherever it was." ([Ns], [703s-706s])

## 10. Concrete numbers

**Rule: The risk multiple for a momentum trader is 2 (risk 1 to gain 2).**
> "my risk multiple is two." ([Ns], [1071s-1072s])
> "if we're risking one hundred dollars, I'm looking to gain about two hundred dollars." ([Ns], [1097s-1100s])

**Rule: A scalper might have a 1:1 risk-to-reward.**

**Rule: A momentum trader might have 10 consecutive losses, but 5 good trades will make up for them.**
