# Short Playbook ("Elevator Down") — Criteria Differences Shorts vs Longs

All criteria differences between short selling and long trading in the Scarface/J-Dub break-and-retest system.

---

## 1. ENTRY AGGRESSIVENESS — THE CORE DIFFERENCE

**Shorts = aggressive entries, limit orders at the level, no confirmation candle.**

> "Elev stair step up elevator down. So a lot of times when I am trading towards the downside I'm going to be a little bit more aggressive where if I'm trading towards the upside I'm going to wait for extra confirmation."
> — J-Dub, `xJ521HB06gg_transcript.txt`, [1108s-1129s]

> "It's that's why the downside is a little bit more discretionary because it is you have to be a little bit more aggressive."
> — J-Dub, `xJ521HB06gg_transcript.txt`, [1697s-1702s]

> "Generally speaking just because right upside loves confirmation candles downside... Sometimes you'll see it taps it perfectly... Rejects and then it does if you wait for the confirmation candle on the downside most of the times it's already at low of days. So the risk for reward doesn't make too much sense."
> — J-Dub, `xJ521HB06gg_transcript.txt`, [1118s-1129s]

> "I personally like shorting as well, but they're just more aggressive. You'll see, as I mentioned, it's always a kind of stair step up, elevator down. So when you're on the short side, for me personally, you have to be a little bit more aggressive. So a lot of times if you're not aggressive right off the bat, you're going to miss that short position."
> — J-Dub, `H8L_aspf3u0_transcript.txt`, [355s-399s]

**Longs = wait for confirmation candle, less aggressive.**

> "As for the long side, we can always wait for that confirmation candle."
> — J-Dub, `H8L_aspf3u0_transcript.txt`, [3461s-3472s]

**Puts (short-side options) can use more aggressive entries. Calls need more criteria.**

> "Coach confirms YES — separate playbooks for PDH vs PDL. Puts can use more aggressive entries; calls need more criteria."
> — Neto (Performance Coach), `coaching 2945210`, [00:21:24]

## 2. NUMBER OF ENTRIES AVAILABLE

**Short side: usually ONE entry, then price dumps.**

> "A lot of times for the short side, it usually offers you one entry and then just kind of dumps, right? Whereas the long side, that's why we like it so much is because sometimes it offers you a few entries, you can wait for confirmation in this case, right?"
> — J-Dub, `H8L_aspf3u0_transcript.txt`, [3586s-3594s]

**Long side: multiple entries available.**

> "Whereas the long side, that's why we like it so much is because sometimes it offers you a few entries, you can wait for confirmation."
> — J-Dub, `H8L_aspf3u0_transcript.txt`, [3586s-3594s]

---

## 3. ENTRY TYPE — LIMIT ORDER VS CONFIRMATION CANDLE

**Short side: limit order (most aggressive) works best.**

> "So I think the limit order, like the most aggressive one for the short side works the best. You have to be a lot more aggressive on the short side."
> — J-Dub, `H8L_aspf3u0_transcript.txt`, [3465s-3467s]

> "So when shorting you usually use the break and retest or just enter on the break... it's usually more like aggressive, like immediate limit orders and then just kind of pushes towards the downside."
> — J-Dub, `H8L_aspf3u0_transcript.txt`, [3554s-3562s]

**Short entry method:** Immediate limit order at the level. Don't wait for candle close confirmation.

---

## 4. DISPLACEMENT REQUIREMENTS

**Longs: displacement required.** Classic B&R needs clear impulsive move away from level.

> "What you want is a little bit more displacement."
> — `obSmDLZ9icM_transcript.txt`, [855s-869s]

> "Really no displacement right here. So not really the greatest trade."
> — Accelerator `1450449`, [00:57:55]

**Shorts: displacement may NOT be needed on rejection.**

> "Separate playbooks for long vs short — puts can use more aggressive entries (no displacement needed on rejection)."
> — `scarface-rules-coaching-bonus.md` Headline Finding (line 12)

**Rationale:** Short side doesn't need an impulsive move away first. Entry on rejection at the level itself. The "elevator down" pattern means if you wait for displacement, you've missed the move.

## 8. STOP PLACEMENT — SAME RULES FOR BOTH

No structural difference in stop placement between long and short:

> "Stop loss = the impulsive candle that broke out. Break below that candle."
> — `bonus_1461019`, [00:07:24]

> "Risk level is always break of key level or break of trend/market structure."
> — `bonus_1460330`, [00:20:40]

**Difference:** On short side, stop triggers faster because price moves faster. Must enter more aggressively to get any R:R at all.

---

## 9. TARGET DIFFERENCES — SAME STRUCTURE, DIFFERENT ECONOMICS

Both use same target hierarchy:
- First target = HOD (longs) / LOD (shorts) — always scale there
- Then external liquidity: PDH/PDL, gap fill, psych numbers, old highs/lows

> "For me, it is I will exit always at high of day slash low of day. That is always one scale."
> — `qjItmSl400E_transcript.txt`, [243s-269s]

**Implicit difference:** Short side moves faster. If you wait for confirmation on short side:
> "If you wait for the confirmation candle on the downside most of the times it's already at low of days. So the risk for reward doesn't make too much sense."
> — J-Dub, `xJ521HB06gg_transcript.txt`, [1118s-1129s]

## CONSOLIDATED SUMMARY

| Dimension | Longs | Shorts |
|-----------|-------|--------|
| Entry style | Wait for confirmation | Limit order, immediate |
| Entry type | Break + retest + candle close | Rejection at level / reclaim break |
| Displacement | Required | Not needed (rejection entry) |
| # of entries | Multiple (stair-step up) | Usually one (elevator down) |
| Entry candle | Hammer | Inverted hammer / shooting star |
| Market structure | PDH break | PDL break |
| R:R risk | Can wait, still good R:R | Waiting destroys R:R |
| Discretion | More mechanical | More discretionary |
