[5m] trading-floor.json | Hayden | 2025-08-28T20:25:30
  - Candle Close Stop Loss
  
  Pros:
  
  Filters out “wick hunts” and intraday noise.
  
  Keeps you in trades that recover quickly after a fake-out.
  
  Helps align with structure rather than random ticks.
  
  Cons:
  
  Risk can be larger than expected, since by the time the candle closes, the option premium may be down much more.
  
  Hard to execute in options intraday because option premiums are extremely sensitive (gamma, delta shifts). A stock moving $0.30 against you can crush the premium more than expected.
  
  Can lead to hesitating, hoping the candle “saves” the trade, which sometimes just means deeper loss.
  
  
  
  - Hard Stop at Exact Price
  
  Pros:
  
  Clear, mechanical, no decision fatigue.
  
  Protects capital better, especially in options where theta + gamma magnify losses quickly.
  
  Removes emotional “what if it comes back?”
  
  Cons:
  
  You’ll get wicked out more often, especially intraday.
  
  Slippage can hurt — a 10–20¢ slip in stock can translate into 20–30% option loss.
  
  Misses some trades that recover immediate
