## (a) DAY-TRADE DELTAS

**1. Opening Drive Play (new setup type)**
"opening drive play... what you really want to see is a strong trend day to either side... in the pre-market or before market closes you want to see consolidation or a pullback... at market open essentially what you want to see is an opening drive play a pop and drop a dip and rip... entry model in this case would have been that opening range in the five minutes" (92E4rnrCewE, [1106s-1130s])
*Delta: New setup type not covered in existing rulebooks. Existing rules cover break-and-retest and opening candle retest, but this is a distinct "opening drive" pattern requiring prior-day trend + pre-market consolidation + opening-range break.*

**2. Gap Fill Trade (new setup type)**
"mark out your gap... you want to see price break inside the gap retest the bottom of your gap... which also coincides with your opening candle print stop above and then your targets would be that gap fill" (92E4rnrCewE, [1409s-1420s])
*Delta: New setup type. Existing rules mention gap fill as a target, but not as a distinct entry setup with specific structure (break inside gap → retest bottom → entry).*

**3. Immediate Rebalance (advanced break-and-retest variant)**
"immediate rebalance... as soon as it gets below this level... the very next candle right immediate retest towards the downside... this signals that price is super weak... it does not have any displacement whatsoever... this is more of an advanced one" (92E4rnrCewE, [2999s-3034s])
*Delta: New variant of break-and-retest where there is NO displacement/FVG — price immediately retests and continues. Existing rules require displacement/FVG for valid break-and-retest.*

**4. Scalping vs A+ Setup Distinction (playbook differentiation)**
"when we're developing our playbook it's a lot easier to develop a playbook for your a plus setup... scalping things can happen so quickly... these type of trades I typically try and avoid as best as possible... I'll have around like a 50% win rate... I'll make money a little bit in trending markets... I'll lose money in choppy markets" (92E4rnrCewE, [899s-1703s])
*Delta: Explicitly states scalping trades have ~50% win rate and should be avoided in choppy markets. Existing rules don't quantify scalp win rates or explicitly advise avoiding scalps in certain market conditions.*

**5. Entry Timing: Confirmation vs Aggressive**
"if you wait for confirmation... you're going to have a higher win rate but you're going to have a lower risk to reward ratio... if you want a higher risk reward ratio with lower win rate you can buy in as soon as it taps this key level" (92E4rnrCewE, [2742s-2814s])
*Delta: Explicit trade-off between confirmation (higher win rate, lower R:R) vs aggressive entry (lower win rate, higher R:R). Existing rules only describe the confirmation-based entry.*

**6. No Entry After 11am (refinement)**
"you don't want to take an entry after 11 unless... a plus setup... or the market's trending... besides that on regular days, you kind of want to get away" (92E4rnrCewE, [1832s-1859s])
*Delta: Refines existing "stop-after-win" informal rule into a hard time cutoff (11am) with two exceptions (A+ setup or trending market).*

**7. Power Hour Trading = Higher Timeframe**
"when you're trading later in the day, you're going to be stuck on the five minute time frame... you won't be shaken out as easily... I would advise you to stick onto the five minute time frame where it's cleaner price action" (92E4rnrCewE, [1878s-1903s])
*Delta: Specific guidance for power hour — switch to 5m timeframe. Existing rules don't address timeframe selection for power hour.*

**8. Fair Value Gap as Displacement Definition**
"a valid displacement is when you have a fair value gap... when the bottom of the wick on the first candle is not overlapping the top of the wick on the third candle" (92E4rnrCewE, [2944s-2976s])
*Delta: Explicitly defines displacement as FVG (three-candle sequence with non-overlapping wicks). Existing rules mention displacement but don't define it this precisely.*

## (b) SWING / LONG-TERM

**1. Swing Position Target = Previous Day Low**
"anyone that's looking to swing this position towards the downside... most likely all swing traders... would hold this overnight or maybe sell very close to close... clear targets... previous day low" (92E4rnrCewE, [1063s-1092s])
*Delta: Swing traders target previous day low as profit zone. Not covered in existing swing rules.*

**2. Swing Trader Profit-Taking at Key Levels**
"where are they going to look to sell if they held their position overnight? They're going to look to sell anywhere below or around previous day low... that's where they're going to take profits if they're trend traders" (92E4rnrCewE, [1231s-1243s])
*Delta: Swing/trend traders specifically take profits at previous day low. Existing swing rules don't specify this target.*

**3. Continuation Trading Requires Higher Timeframe Context**
"if price is going to continue... what's happening on the higher time frame? That's the main thing... if you're only fixated on the one minute time frame... you wouldn't know if price is going to gravitate lower" (92E4rnrCewE, [2059s-2169s])
*Delta: For continuation trades, must check HTF context — not just 1m chart. Existing rules don't explicitly link continuation to HTF analysis.*

**4. V-Shape Recovery is Rare**
"when have you ever seen price do this... big push down and just end up having a v shape recovery? Very rarely... that doesn't happen too often" (92E4rnrCewE, [2196s-2206s])
*Delta: After a strong directional move, V-shape reversal is unlikely — can hold through pullbacks. Not covered in existing rules.*

**5. Partial Taking on Continuation**
"you always want to be taking partials... you always want to lock in profits... if you can lock in profits you're able to hold for those pullbacks and you're able to hold for those bigger momentum moves" (92E4rnrCewE, [2034s-2042s])
*Delta: Taking partials enables holding through pullbacks for continuation. Existing rules mention scaling out but not this rationale for holding.*

**6. A+ Setup = All Timeframes Aligned**
"a plus is when all time frames are aligned... it's based off of the higher time frame setups" (92E4rnrCewE, [2911s-2916s])
*Delta: Defines A+ setup as multi-timeframe alignment. Existing rules mention A/A+ grading but don't define it this specifically.*

**7. First Scale Target = 1 Hour After Entry**
"my best technically first scale is like one hour after I enter" (92E4rnrCewE, [2903s-2907s])
*Delta: Specific timing for first partial profit target on A+ setups. Not covered in existing rules.*

**8. Swing Position Sizing (20-30k)**
"Tony will have like a 20-30 thousand dollar position size... no problem getting in 20-30 thousand dollar position size if you're trading with the liquid names" (92E4rnrCewE, [2636s-2641s])
*Delta: Specific position sizing for swing/larger trades. Existing rules don't specify dollar amounts.*

**9. Momentum Trader vs Day Trader Distinction**
"as a momentum trader versus like a day trader... it's a completely different setup... we usually take one out the money... because we're momentum and that's where our target's usually at... we always target high day or low of day... whereas if you're day trader... you're looking for key pivot level" (92E4rnrCewE, [3246s-3272s])
*Delta: Distinguishes momentum trading (targets HOD/LOD, uses OTM options) from day trading (targets pivot levels). Existing rules don't make this distinction.*
