## (a) DAY-TRADE DELTAS

**1. Scalping definition refinement (5-10 min vs sub-minute)**
"my definition of scalping is anything around like very short period of time. So usually like around five to 10 minutes. That's what I would consider scalping personally. A couple of people talk about scalping, like maybe less than a minute." (6o0XFgLEHp8, [124s])

**2. Scalping frequency depends on market "heat"**
"I don't really like to scalp that often... It's only when if the market's like really, if the market's really hot, I'll look to scalp more, right? Because there's more opportunity. There's more liquidity. But if the market's really slow, I really don't like to look for scouts because the opportunities load, there's less liquidity in the markets." (6o0XFgLEHp8, [235s])

**3. Gap fill trades are lower probability than opening drive**
"These gap fill trades are a little bit more difficult to hit, right? Especially these gap fill ones. So that's why I like talking about the opening drive because those ones are a lot more clear, right? And those ones are a lot more high probability." (6o0XFgLEHp8, [2095s])

**4. Gap fill trades have limited upside (50% max)**
"the contracts in this case, they maybe were only up like, I think it was only up like 50% max. So that's like the maximum this trade would have gave you." (6o0XFgLEHp8, [2110s])

**5. 10am pullback pattern (macro zone)**
"right around 10 o'clock, usually there's a lot of reversals that happen around 10 o'clock because there's a macro zone 10 minutes before, 10 minutes after, a lot of injection of liquidity. Usually if there's a push one way around 10 o'clock, you'll have some sort of pullback, right?" (6o0XFgLEHp8, [1012s])

**6. Opening drive play - if doesn't work immediately, cut**
"if it doesn't work right away, I basically have to cut it... if it doesn't go up right away, you essentially basically have to cut your position, or you can wait for like a five minute candle closure. But majority of the time, right, if it doesn't work right away, you have to cut it." (6o0XFgLEHp8, [1131s])

**7. Gap fill trade - do NOT short after big gap down in uptrend**
"Whenever you see this, do not look for shorts whatsoever, right? Whenever, especially on things like this, whenever you're in such a big uptrend on the higher time frames... you have news like this that's towards the downside, 90% of the time it ends up getting bought back up." **8. Gap fill within 48 hours**
"Majority of the time, right? Price ends up filling this position, like ends up coming back and filling this gap within a 48 hour period." (6o0XFgLEHp8, [1552s])

**9. Order block drawing: wick-to-body is strongest, but use body when no top wick**

**10. Prefer order blocks over fair value gaps for tech names**
"Usually I like to use order blocks, especially if you're trading tech names. I don't really like to use fair value gaps too much. There's some cases I will use fair value gaps if there's no order blocks, but majority of the time I'm 90% order blocks." (6o0XFgLEHp8, [2967s])

**13. Counter-trend trading (gap fill) is micro uptrend in larger downtrend**
"That scalp is essentially a pullback from a higher time frame downtrend, exactly, right? So on the higher time frames, right? So on the higher time frames, it's in a downtrend, but on the lower time frames, you're technically in a micro uptrend." (6o0XFgLEHp8, [2053s])
