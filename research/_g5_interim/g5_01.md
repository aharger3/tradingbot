## (a) DAY-TRADE DELTAS

**1. Break-and-retest refinement: "First touch" vs "pullback" distinction**
- "The first time price comes up to a key level, right? You don't really want to be buying the break and retest off a higher date because logically speaking, everyone that's buying down here, right? Where are they going to look to sell? They're going to look to sell at all-time highs. So you kind of don't want to be buying the break and retest off of at this all-time high level where you kind of want to wait for a pullback." (SO38Y2XyLzU, [727s-745s])
- **Delta**: Existing rulebook says "break validity → retest → entry." This adds a **first-touch avoidance rule**: on the first touch of a major level (especially ATH), do NOT buy the break-and-retest; wait for a pullback first, then a second break with displacement. Contradicts the implicit assumption that any valid break-and-retest is tradeable.

**2. Displacement not required for break-and-retest (contradiction)**
- "You don't have to have displacement on a break and retest. You'll see a lot of the times, right, price will have an uptrend. Sometimes it doesn't have displacement and you'll see it just kind of bases and then launches straight towards the upside." (SO38Y2XyLzU, [549s-560s])
- **Delta**: Existing rulebook treats displacement as a core component of break validity. This explicitly states displacement is **optional** — especially in strong trends or near open. Contradicts the "break validity requires displacement" rule.

**3. "Open and dry" play — immediate retest without displacement**
- "A lot of times you'll see this happens right off the bat, right? Within the first 30 minutes, a lot of times price will kind of just immediate rebounds, right? It'll just show super strength. It'll just tap a key level on the one minute time frame, maybe have like 10, 20 seconds to enter and then just launches towards the upside. Right? That's also a break and retest as well." (SO38Y2XyLzU, [564s-577s])
- **Delta**: New rule: **"Open and dry" break-and-retest** — within first 30 min, price may tap a key level and launch immediately with no displacement. Entry window is 10-20 seconds. This is a distinct subtype not covered in existing rulebook.

**4. Order block drawing: "wick to body" is highest probability**
- "Every single time, the highest probability order blocks, right, are going to happen from the work to the body. Every single time, if you have a high probability order block, it's going to happen from the work to the body, right? Work to the body is your highest probability order block." (SO38Y2XyLzU, [1303s-1314s])
- **Delta**: Existing rulebook says "order blocks = down-close candles, body strength." This adds a **specific drawing rule**: the highest-probability bounce zone is from the top of the wick to the top of the body (not the full candle). Stop goes at the bottom of the full candle. Refines the existing order block rule.

**5. Continuation order block vs reversal order block**
- "There's the classic order block where everyone talks about like essentially like a reversal order block. Or I trade, what I personally trade is the continuation order block. So for the reversal order block, you need the break of structure, you need the market structure shift. But for a continuation order block, right? All you need is essentially a candle closure above that level." (SO38Y2XyLzU, [1773s-1793s])
- **Delta**: New distinction: **continuation order block** (no market structure shift needed, just close above level) vs **reversal order block** (requires break of structure). Existing rulebook only covers the continuation variant implicitly. This adds a formal classification.

**6. Do NOT mark order blocks on pre-market**
- "Don't market order blocks on the pre-market. Why? Because it's just not, you can market order blocks on the pre-market. Obviously, I don't really like drawing out, the only levels I have will mark out on the pre-market are just key levels" (SO38Y2XyLzU, [3202s-3210s])
- **Delta**: New rule: **Pre-market order blocks are invalid** — only draw key levels (not order blocks) in pre-market. Contradicts any assumption that order blocks can be drawn on pre-market data.

**7. "If price doesn't come back to retest, that's bullish"**
- "If price doesn't come back towards essentially your retest levels, your key breaking retest, your key order blocks, that's when you know price is so bullish and price is in a hurry for a move towards the upside." (SO38Y2XyLzU, [3116s-3128s])
- **Delta**: New diagnostic: **Failure to retest = extreme bullishness**, not a missed setup. Existing rulebook treats retest as required for entry; this says absence of retest is itself a signal of trend strength.
