## (a) DAY-TRADE DELTAS

**1. Gap-fill statistics (contradicts "gaps always fill" assumption)**
> "I talk about this being I'm pretty sure yeah it'll fill 40 and fill 70% of the time in a 48 hour period"
(Ng5MJTUFldg_transcript.txt, [343s-348s])
*Delta:* Explicit statistic: gaps fill ~70% within 48 hours. Contradicts any assumption that gaps always fill immediately.

**2. Breakaway gap concept (new rule refinement)**
> "I mentioned what I want to see this turn into is a breakaway gap meaning that price does not return back into the gap level... this only happens when we have some sort of external news event"
(Ng5MJTUFldg_transcript.txt, [370s-415s])
*Delta:* New concept not in existing rulebooks. Breakaway gap = gap that does NOT fill, requires external news catalyst. This is distinct from standard gap-fill expectation.

**3. First-move fakeout on regular days (contradicts opening play bias)**
> "on a regular day right if you're looking at the spine cues you'll see the spine cues on a regular day or majority of times the first move is usually kind of like that at least the first very small move is usually that first kind of like fake move in the overall market"
(Ng5MJTUFldg_transcript.txt, [1890s-1902s])
*Delta:* On regular (non-news) days, the first move is often a fakeout. Contradicts the general "opening play" bias that assumes first move is directional.

**4. High-of-day scaling rule refinement (contradicts "scale at HOD" assumption)**
> "if I'm looking to scale at high day I probably won't try and take the trade because it's not worth it to me... unless you have a nice bigger retracement"
(Ng5MJTUFldg_transcript.txt, [3415s-3428s])
*Delta:* Explicitly avoids scaling at HOD unless entry was on a 5-minute pullback with good risk/reward. Contradicts any rule that says "always scale at HOD."

**5. Position sizing across tickers (new risk management rule)**
> "you're not going to be trading the same contracts on the size right it's the dollar amount... if you trade the exact same position size which is two contracts where your risk is going to be drastically different"
(Ng5MJTUFldg_transcript.txt, [1632s-1674s])
*Delta:* New rule: Position size must be dollar-equivalent across tickers, not contract-equivalent. Tesla vs Apple example shows 4x risk discrepancy.

**6. IV/theta impact on stop-loss (new nuance)**
> "the reason why I was down so much on this trade was because the actual options contract the IV was fairly juiced higher and it ended up right it was basically that theta that kind of wrecked the contracts"
(Ng5MJTUFldg_transcript.txt, [2286s-2294s])
*Delta:* Stop-loss on underlying may not protect against IV/theta decay in options. New consideration for option-specific risk management.

## (b) SWING / LONG-TERM

**1. Weekly profile = fractal of opening range (new swing framework)**
> "it's very similar like the weekly profile daily profile all the way down towards the one minute all the candles are they always the exact same right we always look for basically opening candle right break above retest and then continuation back towards upside"
(Ng5MJTUFldg_transcript.txt, [1416s-1429s])
*Verbatim:* The weekly/daily profile mirrors the 1-minute opening range pattern: dip → reclaim → retest → continuation.

**2. Swing entry on first major dip after news event (new swing setup)**
> "first dip you expect that first major dip to get bought up... Tesla first dip as well was also like a super high quality setup as well right broke above first dip kind of get bought and then eventually it would have taken a little bit longer but right would have been like a swing position"
(Ng5MJTUFldg_transcript.txt, [2348s-2386s])
*Verbatim:* After major news event (elections/earnings), the first major dip is a high-quality swing entry. Requires holding through some volatility.

**3. Swing thesis = HTF break-and-retest, not opening play (new distinction)**
> "this one trade all the way down here would have been a completely different thesis being that like higher time frame break and retest so the actual trade in itself the opening play I'll take that trade right like I'd say like nine out of ten times... this trade right it's more of like a swing trade basically off holding your 478"
(Ng5MJTUFldg_transcript.txt, [2309s-2334s])
*Verbatim:* Distinguishes between opening play (scalp, 9/10 times) vs swing (HTF break-and-retest, different thesis). Swing requires holding through pullbacks.

**4. Swing scaling strategy (new detailed framework)**
> "you can go back until lower time frames and mark out other key levels... let's say you sell 25 percent right there... let's say you sell another 25 so now you only have 50 percent of your position left... and then you're 25 left right in this case since price is very much bullish right you can either have a hard stop when you're such far in the money right you can kind of just have a hard stop and just let it play out"
(Ng5MJTUFldg_transcript.txt, [2867s-3074s])
*Verbatim:* Swing scaling: 25% at first key level, 25% at next, 25% at next, leave 25% with hard stop. Use order blocks as support levels for trailing.

**5. Swing = HTF thesis, not lower-timeframe scalping (new rule)**
> "if you have a higher time frame thesis right you want to be looking for those higher time frame objectives the only reason you're going to be on the lower time frames is to get in entries"
(Ng5MJTUFldg_transcript.txt, [3149s-3156s])
*Verbatim:* Swing traders use lower timeframes ONLY for entries, not for exits. Exits are based on HTF objectives.

**6. Swing position management: alerts + walk away (new technique)**
> "you can just walk away and just have like alert side right you can put alerts on trading view you're well in the money on the contract so you don't really expect contracts to come all the way back down"
(Ng5MJTUFldg_transcript.txt, [3060s-3074s])
*Verbatim:* For swing positions deep in the money, set alerts and walk away. Don't micromanage.

**7. Swing selection: consolidation-not-pullback = strength (new criterion)**
> "look at all this consolidation that we had really since like 2023 right this is i remember back here this was in july this had nice price action but quickly kind of sold back off but now finally look how big these like last weeks are and of course we have the major news event"
(Ng5MJTUFldg_transcript.txt, [686s-701s])
*Verbatim:* Long consolidation (2023 to present) followed by news catalyst = strong swing setup. Consolidation-not-pullback indicates accumulation.

**8. Swing targets: old pivot highs after all-time highs (new target framework)**
> "every single pullback now we're going to be using like Fibonacci's right key psych level since technically all the objectives at least towards this area have been met so now when we're ever at all-time highs we can just use key fibs and yeah key psych levels for targets now or we'll always look for these old pivot highs"
(Ng5MJTUFldg_transcript.txt, [492s-512s])
*Verbatim:* After all-time highs are reached, swing targets shift to: (1) Fibonacci extensions, (2) key psychological levels, (3) old pivot highs.

**9. Swing = trend-following, not counter-trend (new rule)**
> "we trade the trend right we don't really like playing like these like as soon as it comes up to a key level we look to kind of anticipate the short towards the downside that usually doesn't happen as often"
(Ng5MJTUFldg_transcript.txt, [1100s-1113s])
*Verbatim:* Swing = trend-following only. Do not anticipate reversals at key levels. Wait for dips to buy, not peaks to short.

**10. Swing = patience for major dips, not minor pullbacks (new entry criterion)**
> "i personally would rather wait for major dips where you can look to long it because like we trade the trend right we don't really like playing like these like as soon as it comes up to a key level we look to kind of anticipate the short"
(Ng5MJTUFldg_transcript.txt, [1098s-1113s])
*Verbatim:* Swing entries require "major dips" (significant pullbacks to key levels), not minor intraday pullbacks.

**11. Swing = no time frame for targets (new mindset)**
> "there's no like specific time frame right we're just going to play what's in front of us right we're we basically day traders swing traders anyways we're at least long-term investing right you're you don't really care about the time that it goes right as long as it goes up"
(Ng5MJTUFldg_transcript.txt, [1263s-1278s])
*Verbatim:* Swing traders should not have time-based targets. Only directional bias matters.

**12. Swing = liquidity injection catalyst (new requirement)**
> "whenever we do have any sort of news event or anything along those lines we always have an injection of volatility and liquidity and that's all what we need"
(Ng5MJTUFldg_transcript.txt, [1577s-1581s])
*Verbatim:* Swing setups require a liquidity injection catalyst (news event). Without it, price action is choppy and un-tradeable.

**13. Swing = weekly/daily levels, not intraday (new level hierarchy)**
> "these ones like these weekly ones daily ones right since it's quite large you can mark out the daily ones as well and these ones will use as objectives back towards the upside"
(Ng5MJTUFldg_transcript.txt, [1011s-1017s])
*Verbatim:* Swing objectives are weekly and daily levels, not intraday levels. Mark them on higher timeframes.

**14. Swing = hold through pullbacks (new holding rule)**
> "you would have had to hold through quite a bit but eventually it would end up gravitated higher based off the higher time frame thesis"
(Ng5MJTUFldg_transcript.txt, [2390s-2395s])
*Verbatim:* Swing positions require holding through significant pullbacks. Thesis must be strong enough to withstand drawdown.

**15. Swing = no shorting in strong uptrend (new rule)**
> "if you go and try and short this you're gonna lose majority of the time right on this push towards upside if anyone was trying to short this right it would have been very hard to make money"
(Ng5MJTUFldg_transcript.txt, [1068s-1084s])
*Verbatim:* In strong uptrends (post-news), do not attempt short swings. Only look for long entries on dips.
