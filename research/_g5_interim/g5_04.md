## (a) DAY-TRADE DELTAS

**1. Internal vs. External Liquidity Framework (Refinement to existing B&R/OB rules)**
- "If we're looking for external liquidity, we need an internal liquidity entry model, which is your order blocks... or a break and retest... pre market high, pre market low retest... opening range candles, opening range break retest" (ZFPnTRx_gQI_transcript.txt, [363s-398s])
- **Delta:** Formalizes that ALL entry models (OB, B&R, opening range retest, PMH/PML retest) are "internal liquidity" tools used to target "external liquidity" (PDH/PDL, weekly highs/lows). This is a structural refinement — existing rules treat B&R/OB as standalone setups, not as a unified internal-liquidity-seeking framework.

**2. Reversal vs. Continuation Trade Classification (Contradiction to "market designed to go up" bias)**
- "Internal liquidity to external liquidity is a lot of times like continuation... Whereas if you're going from external liquidity to internal liquidity, this is more of a reversal trade" (ZFPnTRx_gQI_transcript.txt, [451s-464s])
- "Rarely will you see us trade gas fills... this is like a reversal trade... Majority of our setups will come when there is previous day high, previous day low, and we'll look for... continuation" (ZFPnTRx_gQI_transcript.txt, [447s-552s])
- **Delta:** Explicitly classifies gap-fill trades as reversals (external→internal) and says they are rarely taken. Existing rules mention gap fills but don't classify them as reversal trades or state they're low-priority.

**3. Inside Bar Day as Liquidity-Building Setup (New rule not in existing rulebooks)**
- "Inside bar day... means liquidity is business... price the third day... if it breaks underneath this previous... low... gravitate towards the low of the first day... if it breaks towards the upside... gravitate towards the top of the outer bar" (ZFPnTRx_gQI_transcript.txt, [660s-771s])
- "How to trade the inside bar is as soon as price breaks above, you want to basically look for the retest... stop just to break below... target this liquidity" (ZFPnTRx_gQI_transcript.txt, [788s-805s])
- "Works best on the daily chart... best for continuations in a trend" (ZFPnTRx_gQI_transcript.txt, [864s-883s])
- **Delta:** Entirely new setup — three-bar inside bar pattern on daily chart. Not covered in existing rulebooks.

**4. Gap-and-Go Criteria (Refinement to gap-fill rules)**
- "If you're looking for the gap and go, you want to gap above a key resistance level... and you want to see price really gravitate right off the start... super bullish right off the start" (ZFPnTRx_gQI_transcript.txt, [1547s-1560s])
- "If you look at any gap and go setups... gap around above a super key area... earnings... bullish on this name... triple top resistance... flip right off the bat" (ZFPnTRx_gQI_transcript.txt, [1626s-1678s])
- **Delta:** Adds specific criteria for gap-and-go (vs. gap-fill): gap above key resistance, immediate bullish momentum, earnings catalyst, multiple resistance levels. Existing rules only cover gap-fill mechanics.

**5. A+ Setup Failure Rate (New judgment criterion)**
- "If you actually have an A plus setup... they'll rarely, rarely fail... like 90% of the time... But those setups don't come as often into the markets" (ZFPnTRx_gQI_transcript.txt, [3148s-3167s])
- **Delta:** Quantifies A+ success rate (~90%) and rarity. Existing rules define A+ criteria but don't give failure-rate stats.

**6. News Day Caution (Refinement to existing news-day rule)**
- "Whenever we do have news, a lot of times what we'll have is kind of reversals in the markets... you have to be very, very careful, especially for gapping up" (ZFPnTRx_gQI_transcript.txt, [1335s-1347s])
- "If it was... no news and there wasn't any external confluences, this could have gravitated towards the upside, but we had news at 830, which kind of... ruin the setup" (ZFPnTRx_gQI_transcript.txt, [1564s-1574s])
- **Delta:** Specifies that news days cause reversals even on gap-and-go setups — existing rule only says "low prob" without this mechanism.

**7. Scaling Out Based on Liquidity Type (Refinement to exit rules)**
- "If you're looking for gap fills... internal liquidity, I always scale majority of my positions at those key levels. If we're looking at continuations and we have higher timeframe objectives, I'm going to be holding majority of my position" (ZFPnTRx_gQI_transcript.txt, [1740s-1749s])
- **Delta:** Exit strategy depends on whether targeting internal (scale out at key level) vs. external liquidity (hold majority). Existing exit rules don't differentiate by liquidity type.

**8. VWAP Not Useful Early in Day (New rule)**
- "Indicators aren't really used right off the bat because there's not too much information... I only use the indicators like later into the day... much later into the day" (ZFPnTRx_gQI_transcript.txt, [2090s-2133s])
- **Delta:** Explicitly says VWAP/indicators are unreliable at market open. Existing rules don't address indicator timing.

**9. Higher Timeframe Level Visibility (Refinement to key level selection)**
- "If you draw... a weekly high... everyone can see this on a chart... whereas if you mark out smaller timeframe levels... not everyone's going to have marked out this level" (ZFPnTRx_gQI_transcript.txt, [2335s-2404s])
- **Delta:** Adds "universal visibility" as criterion for key level importance — levels visible on multiple timeframes are higher probability. Existing rules just say "key levels" without this multi-timeframe visibility test.

**10. Gap Fill Statistics (New rule)**
- "Within a 48 hour periods, gaps fill like 70% of the time" (ZFPnTRx_gQI_transcript.txt, [2458s-2463s])
- **Delta:** Quantifies gap-fill probability (70% within 48h). Existing rules mention gap fills but not this statistic.

## (b) SWING / LONG-TERM

**1. Higher Timeframe Point of Interest as Primary (Swing thesis foundation)**
- "Understanding the higher timeframe is the single most important concept you need to understand as a trader... if you understand where the higher timeframe is going to head... your entry really doesn't matter" (ZFPnTRx_gQI_transcript.txt, [47s-112s])
- "Our best trades almost always occur on the higher timeframes... whenever we do have those higher timeframe home run A plus setups... they always start off on the higher timeframes" (ZFPnTRx_gQI_transcript.txt, [154s-175s])

**2. Draw on Liquidity Framework (Swing direction determination)**
- "Asking yourself, is price going to head higher or lower?... External liquidity or internal liquidity?... price seeking liquidity... old highs, old lows... higher timeframe objectives" (ZFPnTRx_gQI_transcript.txt, [211s-270s])
- "If we're close to all-time highs... you expect the markets to gravitate to... all-time highs... that's where liquidity is resting" (ZFPnTRx_gQI_transcript.txt, [2615s-2620s])

**3. Stock Selection Based on Higher Timeframe Trend (Swing stock picking)**

- "Nvidia... all we've been doing is just buying dips on Nvidia... because Nvidia is in a trending market... super easy to read... All you have to do is just buy dips towards the upside" (ZFPnTRx_gQI_transcript.txt, [2917s-2936s])
- "Tesla... hasn't done as much... kind of just been consolidating... you can't really expect too much continuation on Tesla" (ZFPnTRx_gQI_transcript.txt, [2881s-2971s])

**4. Weekly/Daily Level Marking (Swing level identification)**
- "I start off on the higher timeframes... weekly highs... daily high, daily low... previous day high, previous day low... old highs over old lows" (ZFPnTRx_gQI_transcript.txt, [2589s-2610s])

**5. Gap Fill as Swing Objective (Swing target setting)**

**6. Consolidation vs. Expansion (Swing market regime)**

- "Gaps fill more often during consolidating markets... because price is not really looking to seek external liquidity" (ZFPnTRx_gQI_transcript.txt, [2699s-2708s])

**7. Swing Entry via Lower Timeframe (Execution)**

I'm only entering off these order blocks to target those higher timeframe point of interest" (ZFPnTRx_gQI_transcript.txt, [3006s-3042s])
