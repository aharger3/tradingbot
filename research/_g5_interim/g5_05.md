**1. Playbook concept formalized as a structured document with setups, conditions, entry/exit criteria, risk management, and trade management guidelines**

**2. Playbook should be organized in clear/concise format for easy reference during live sessions**
- "organize the playbook in a clear and concise of format making it easy to reference and follow along during live sessions" (6ChwEX15uYI_transcript.txt, [196s])
- DELTA: New operational guidance on playbook format.

**3. Start with 5 key setups (PDH/PDL retest, PMH/PML retest, opening range break & retest) before expanding**
- "you maybe want to focus like the five key main playbooks... like the previous day high previous day low retest pre-market high pre-market low opening range breaking retest" (6ChwEX15uYI_transcript.txt, [226s])
- DELTA: Specifics on which setups to prioritize first.

**4. Track statistics live (not just backtest) to determine which setups work best for you**
- "I prefer where you have your playbook setup and then you do it in real time, you back test your statistics and then depending on what setup works for you the best then you want to kind of play along to what your best at" (6ChwEX15uYI_transcript.txt, [264s])
- DELTA: New emphasis on live tracking vs. pure backtesting.

**5. Intraday fundamentals (news, earnings, catalysts) are key for A+ setups but less important for scalping**
- "Majority of the time this is very key a lot of people overlook this... majority of the times this actually does play a big key factor into at least higher time frame a plus setups... We're scalping it doesn't really matter too much" (6ChwEX15uYI_transcript.txt, [489s])
- DELTA: Clarifies when fundamentals matter vs. don't.

**6. Divergence (ticker moving opposite to QQQ) is less preferred than relative strength/weakness**
- "I don't really like divergence too much... We personally love trading relative strength and weakness because right if Tesla is pushing higher with the cues... Whereas divergence sometimes... it'll actually end up dragging Tesla and I'll end up killing the trade thesis" (6ChwEX15uYI_transcript.txt, [637s])
- DELTA: Contradicts any assumption that divergence is equally valid — explicitly prefers relative strength/weakness.

**7. News trumps everything — can trade divergence if there's a catalyst**
- "News trumps everything a lot of times... if you have bullish news even though the market could be just consolidating or maybe in a downtrend that day if you have bullish news on one ticker... that's the main catalyst" (6ChwEX15uYI_transcript.txt, [762s])
- DELTA: Exception to the divergence rule.

**8. When above previous day high in pre-market, use PDH as support — don't target it**
- "if we're above previous day high and we're above key levels... we're gonna be looking for the previous day high to use as support... you can't target previous day high and previous day low liquidity if we're above it" (6ChwEX15uYI_transcript.txt, [1005s])
- DELTA: Refinement on PDH/PDL targeting logic.

**9. Don't cut position when close to stop — if anything, add more**
- "There would be no reason for you to exit your position when you're so close to your stop... if anything you would want to add more because this is a key level" (6ChwEX15uYI_transcript.txt, [1209s])
- DELTA: Contradicts conservative "cut losses early" advice — advocates adding at key levels near stop.

**10. Adding near stop = minimal risk contracts (5-10% risk vs 20-30% at entry)**
- "If you buy down here you're risking maybe five per... basically like a little bit of slippage... so you're risking probably like 10% compared to like 20-30% up here" (6ChwEX15uYI_transcript.txt, [1487s])
- DELTA: Specific risk percentages for scaling in near stop.

**11. Inside bar day = liquidity building on both sides, expect opening drive play**
- "Inside bar day means there's just being built up liquidity... we always look to target those previous day high and previous day low levels... Usually a lot of times what offers will be an opening drive play" (6ChwEX15uYI_transcript.txt, [2879s])
- DELTA: New concept — inside bar day as setup for opening drive.

**12. Don't trade inside bar day — high chop risk**

- DELTA: Contradicts "always trade" — explicitly avoid inside bar days.

**13. For scalping, take 80% off at high of day; for HTF setups, scale in quarters**
- "if it's a scalp position... you want to take 80% off and then just leave 10% to ride... Hire time for a setup I personally like scaling in like quarters" (6ChwEX15uYI_transcript.txt, [3216s])
- DELTA: Specific scaling percentages by setup type.

**14. Pre-market preps are essential — don't just come in at 9:30**

**15. Develop "if-then" statements for thesis**
