## (a) DAY-TRADE DELTAS

**1. Opening Play Framework (5 types)**
"there's around five different opening plays... opening drive, dip in rip, pop and drop, gap and go, gap fill... happens within the first 30 minutes of market open" (Q0XC_SuWA4o_transcript.txt, [692s-704s])
*Delta to existing rulebooks:* Adds explicit categorization of opening plays (previously only "opening candle retest" and "break-and-retest" were covered). This is a new structural framework.

**2. Opening Play Requires HTF Objective**
"opening play only happens whenever there's a clear higher time frame objective or there's a clear higher time frame something going on" (Q0XC_SuWA4o_transcript.txt, [744s-748s])
*Delta to existing rulebooks:* Adds condition that opening plays require HTF thesis (existing rules only mention "bias" generally).

**3. Timeframe Selection Based on Opening Play**
"If you have an opening play, you're going to be on the one minute time frame... If you don't have an opening play, then do not be on the one minute time frame" (Q0XC_SuWA4o_transcript.txt, [2289s-2308s])
*Delta to existing rulebooks:* Explicit rule linking timeframe to presence of opening play (existing rules don't specify this conditional timeframe selection).

**4. Relative Strength Only Matters When Extreme**
"relative strength and weakness... it'll really only matters if there's extreme relative strength and weakness when it's like when you can look at the chart for one second and already determined something" (Q0XC_SuWA4o_transcript.txt, [2084s-2090s])
*Delta to existing rulebooks:* Contradicts/refines the general "use relative strength" guidance. Adds threshold: must be immediately obvious.

**5. Five-Minute Opening Range as Secondary Entry**
"if you missed the first entry... your five minute opening range breaking retest... would be the next entry if you missed the first two" (Q0XC_SuWA4o_transcript.txt, [2385s-2399s])
*Delta to existing rulebooks:* Adds hierarchy of entries: 1) opening drive/1m opening range, 2) order block, 3) 5m opening range break-and-retest.

**6. Mechanical Backtesting First, Discretionary Later**
"at the very start you want to back test... as mechanical as possible... then you can go back and look at things that are less mechanical and more discretionary like understanding bias" (Q0XC_SuWA4o_transcript.txt, [1951s-1966s])
*Delta to existing rulebooks:* New process guidance for backtesting methodology.

**7. Key Levels = Old Highs/Old Lows Only**
"majority of my levels come from the daily, the weekly, and the one hour chart... they're always old highs old lows" (Q0XC_SuWA4o_transcript.txt, [1431s-1434s])
*Delta to existing rulebooks:* Clarifies that key levels are exclusively old highs/lows (existing rules mention PDH/PDL, PMH/PML but don't explicitly state this limitation).

**8. Order Block = Wick to Body**
"I always mark out from the wick to the body... that's what's called your order block" (Q0XC_SuWA4o_transcript.txt, [1636s-1637s])
*Delta to existing rulebooks:* Refines order block definition (existing rules mention "body strength" but not wick-to-body marking).

**9. Futures Preferred Over SPX Options**
"I like trading futures personally... I don't really like trading spx options unless I'm taking a little bit longer dated... I don't really like trading zero days" (Q0XC_SuWA4o_transcript.txt, [2564s-2575s])
*Delta to existing rulebooks:* Contradicts general 0DTE rules (existing rules cover 0DTE but don't recommend futures over SPX).

**10. Use Options Price (Not Delta) for Intraday Stops**
"when I'm scalping or I'm day trading... it's easier to look at the options price and have like a general idea of where your risk is... I'll use delta if I'm swinging positions" (Q0XC_SuWA4o_transcript.txt, [2860s-2873s])
*Delta to existing rulebooks:* New guidance on stop calculation method (existing rules don't specify options price vs delta for intraday).

**11. Trade Alone After Learning**
"once you understand the system... you want to go and trade basically on your own... the best is to... form your thesis and then look for setups yourself intraday" (Q0XC_SuWA4o_transcript.txt, [3078s-3126s])
*Delta to existing rulebooks:* New recommendation to trade independently after learning (existing rules don't address this).

## (b) SWING / LONG-TERM

**1. Swing Mentality Different from Day Trading**
"for the swing trades wise the swing trades you have to have a little bit of a different mentality" (Q0XC_SuWA4o_transcript.txt, [388s-389s])

**2. Swing Trades Often Occur in Overnight Sessions**
"a lot of the swing trades in the past... a lot of the best trades have really come actually during those overnight sessions" (Q0XC_SuWA4o_transcript.txt, [398s-401s])

**3. Swing Stop = HTF Level Break**
"If you want to swing it would be something like this... I probably have my stop down here and then have a move all the way back towards all-time highs" (Q0XC_SuWA4o_transcript.txt, [153s-157s])

**4. Swing Entry on Retest of Key Level**
"if you are looking for a swing, you're really gonna be looking for the low risk swing which is all the way risking off your 181 87s or if you want to wait for a little bit more confirmation you can wait for let's say a break back above 188" (Q0XC_SuWA4o_transcript.txt, [619s-627s])

**5. Swing Requires Higher Low Formation**
"if you are gonna look for a swing you want to look for a higher low and you want to see price kind of gravitate back towards upside" (Q0XC_SuWA4o_transcript.txt, [593s-597s])

**6. Swing Sizing for Drawdown**
"you have to size in for basically negative 50 to potentially a little bit higher drawdown because things like... news factors can happen" (Q0XC_SuWA4o_transcript.txt, [1344s-1351s])

**7. Swing Thesis Based on HTF Consolidation**
"from a weekly timeframe perspective it's just kind of consolidating... but it is kind of forming this like downtrend break for a move back towards upside" (Q0XC_SuWA4o_transcript.txt, [91s-99s])

**8. Swing Selection: All-Time Highs = Momentum**
"whenever you're at all-time highs these ones usually have some super solid price action... because they usually have momentum when tickers are at all-time highs" (Q0XC_SuWA4o_transcript.txt, [259s-275s])

**9. Swing Selection: Sympathy/Correlation Plays**
"if I'm expecting the ES and I'm expecting the spy and Qs to push back towards upside that would ultimately help Nvidia push back towards upside as well" (Q0XC_SuWA4o_transcript.txt, [216s-222s])

**10. Swing Selection: Leader-Follower Hierarchy**
"spy is the leader... spy would trade the best towards the upside and then the cues would be the second best... and then if we go into the tech names that would also mean that basically the tech names are also lagging" (Q0XC_SuWA4o_transcript.txt, [469s-489s])

**11. Swing Risk: News Can Disrupt**
"if you are swinging is that sometimes there can be news... that's why you have to be careful" (Q0XC_SuWA4o_transcript.txt, [1335s-1339s])

**12. Swing Setup: Daily Order Block as Support**
"as long as it holds this order block... I expect continuation back towards the highs" (Q0XC_SuWA4o_transcript.txt, [1844s-1884s])

**13. Swing Setup: Downtrend Break Pattern**

**14. Swing Setup: Gap as Support**
"your 181... this was also a previous resistance turning to support level... this is like your low risk for a swing" (Q0XC_SuWA4o_transcript.txt, [570s-581s])

**15. Swing Timeframe: 4H/1H Context**
"if you're on the four hour and you want to swing it, right? You kind of want to see this level hold" (Q0XC_SuWA4o_transcript.txt, [144s-149s])

**16. Swing vs Day Trade: Risk-Reward Difference**
