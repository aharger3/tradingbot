## (a) DAY-TRADE DELTAS

**1. New entry model hierarchy (dip-in-rip > reclaim > opening range break & retest)**
- "One of the best entry models is going to be a retest of some sort of dip coming to market open... This is number one... Number two, you could be looking for the reclaim... And number three, that's basically your retest of your opening candle print... the opening range break and retest" (1S50NNAGTs0, [2793s-2875s])
- **Delta to existing rulebook**: Existing rules only cover break-and-retest and reclaim-based entries. This adds a clear priority ranking: dip-in-rip (best), reclaim (second), opening range break & retest (third/last resort). Also states reclaim is "not usually recommended" and "we don't really teach this one that much" — contradicts the 84% rule's reclaim-as-primary approach.

**2. Opening range break is a "last entry model" when you have a higher timeframe thesis**
- "The opening range break is basically the last entry model, the last potential entry point" (1S50NNAGTs0, [2727s-2732s])
- **Delta to existing rulebook**: Existing rules treat opening range break as a primary entry. This demotes it to last resort when HTF thesis exists.

**3. Scalp vs day-trade time definition (0-30 minutes)**
- "I consider scalp slash day trade... anything between zero to 30 minutes" (1S50NNAGTs0, [2148s-2156s])
- **Delta to existing rulebook**: Existing rules don't define scalp duration. This sets a specific 30-minute boundary.

**4. "Must work right away" rule for 1-minute thesis trades**
- "If it doesn't work right away, I know I'm going to cut my position" (1S50NNAGTs0, [1511s-1513s])
- "They either work right away or they get stopped out right away" (1S50NNAGTs0, [1382s-1385s])
- **Delta to existing rulebook**: Existing 0DTE rules say "must work immediately" but this extends to ALL 1-minute thesis trades regardless of expiry.

**5. Bar-by-bar trailing only after all targets are hit**
- "I only bar by bar when I have no other targets left" (1S50NNAGTs0, [1197s-1198s])
- "Bar by bar is like the last thing I choose... when you're at all time highs" (1S50NNAGTs0, [1331s-1337s])
- **Delta to existing rulebook**: Existing rules mention bar-by-bar as a trailing method but don't specify it's only for post-target management.

**6. Risk management for scalps: general area, not exact amount**
- "When you're scalping... you're never going to have an exact amount... it's going to be like a general area of risk" (1S50NNAGTs0, [979s-1001s])
- **Delta to existing rulebook**: Existing rules discuss risk in terms of stop levels, not this "general area" concept for scalps.

**7. 1-minute thesis = tight stop, 5-minute thesis = deeper stop**
- "If it's a one minute... you're not going to give it that much room... you have a higher timeframe thesis, you can give it a little bit more room" (1S50NNAGTs0, [1619s-1627s])
- **Delta to existing rulebook**: Existing rules don't explicitly tie stop width to entry timeframe.

**8. Day trade only needs 1H + 5M alignment (not weekly/daily)**
- "For a day trade, technically all you need for a day trade is just your one hour and your five minute" (1S50NNAGTs0, [1964s-1968s])
- **Delta to existing rulebook**: Existing rules mention HTF alignment but don't specify this minimum requirement.

**9. 5-minute trend alone is unreliable — easily reverses**
- "A five minute trend is not that like it can easily reverse on you... you don't want to marry your bias" (1S50NNAGTs0, [2110s-2116s])
- **Delta to existing rulebook**: Existing rules don't warn against trading on 5M trend alone without 1H confirmation.

**10. Trade review judgment: "Don't try to guess the top"**
- "Don't try and guess the top because if you try and guess the top, it's going to be very difficult" (1S50NNAGTs0, [750s-752s])
- "If you tried shorting Tesla throughout any of this whole price run, you maybe would have made money on one day... 10% chance" (1S50NNAGTs0, [797s-841s])
- **Delta to existing rulebook**: Existing rules don't explicitly quantify the futility of counter-trend shorts in strong trends.

## (b) SWING / LONG-TERM

**1. Swing trade requires weekly + daily + 1H alignment (5M can be bearish)**
- "For a swing trade, you basically need the weekly, the daily and the one hour... the five minute can be bearish... because you're having a higher timeframe move" (1S50NNAGTs0, [2021s-2038s])
- "You want pullbacks... you really want like a five minute pullback... because as soon as the five minute flips bullish, that's when every single chart is aligning" (1S50NNAGTs0, [2045s-2062s])

**2. Swing entry: buy when 5M is bearish (pullback)**
- "When you want to buy swing trades is really when the five minute is actually bearish because that's when it's going to provide the best trading opportunities" (1S50NNAGTs0, [2039s-2044s])

**3. Swing position management: maintain through intraday pullbacks**
- "I'm just going to maintain... I had like a week and a half till expiration... I'm just going to maintain price comes down... still holds our key areas" (1S50NNAGTs0, [2569s-2578s])

**4. Swing stop = HTF level break (deeper than day trade)**
- "My stop was fairly... it was deeper... I had a swing position on this one... I think I had to stop all the way down here" (1S50NNAGTs0, [1578s-1586s])

**5. Swing selection: "flat top break" = strength**
- "Flat top break... nice clear trade... I'm holding my position... majority of my position... I'm not selling until we hit major targets" (1S50NNAGTs0, [1705s-1715s])

**6. Swing requires weekly/daily alignment — without it, don't swing**
- "If you can't build the thesis on the higher time frames, then you have no business being on the lower time frames" (1S50NNAGTs0, [3036s-3039s])

**7. Swing contract selection: out-of-the-money for targets**
- "If it's a swing trade, maybe a couple out the money" (1S50NNAGTs0, [650s-653s])

**8. Swing thesis: "keep longing until it says otherwise"**
- "You're just going to keep longing until it says otherwise... you're in a clear trend on the higher timeframes, you don't really have to over complicate it" (1S50NNAGTs0, [717s-723s])

**9. Swing judgment: don't sell swing position at intraday highs**
- "I'm not going to sell here because... I've out the money contracts... I'm going to give it a lower room" (1S50NNAGTs0, [2564s-2568s])

**10. Swing vs day trade distinction: swing = all timeframes aligned, day trade = 1H + 5M only**
- "For a good swing trade, you basically need every single trade... weekly to be in check, daily to be in check, one hour to be in check... For a day trade, technically all you need is just your one hour and your five minute" (1S50NNAGTs0, [1951s-1968s])
