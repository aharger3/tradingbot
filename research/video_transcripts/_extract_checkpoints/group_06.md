## 12. Entry Models Summary

### Entry #1: Limit Order / No Confirmation
- **Pros**: Best entry (filled on bid), highest R:R to high of day
- **Cons**: ~30-40% win rate, prone to fakeouts, unnecessary losses
- **Usage**: "We don't use this one as much. There's very, there needs to be a very clear trend and need to be very aggressive in order for me to take the first one." (boot-camp-recordings_Day_3_Technical_Analysis.txt [3136s-3142s])

### Entry #2: Strong Price Action / Buyers Stepping In
- **Pros**: Waiting for buyers, risk closer to swing low, can get in majority of time
- **Cons**: ~50% win rate, still prone to fakeouts, more discretionary
- **Usage**: "This is something we look for every single day." (boot-camp-recordings_Day_3_Technical_Analysis.txt [3148s-3153s])

### Entry #3: Strong Price Action + Confirmation Candle
- **Pros**: Highest win rate (~60-70%), rarely any fakeouts, shortest time in trade
- **Cons**: Worst R:R to high day, sometimes miss trade, buying after bullish candle
- **Usage**: "This is primarily what Tony uses is the strong price action slash buyer step in plus the confirmation candle." (boot-camp-recordings_Day_3_Technical_Analysis.txt [3831s-3835s])

### Downside Entry - Be More Aggressive
- **Stair step up, elevator down**: "stair step up elevator down. Right? So if you ever see price move in an up, upwards line, right? When you're trading towards upside, usually can wait for those candles to close, right? That's what I like to do.
