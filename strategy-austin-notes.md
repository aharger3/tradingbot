# Austin's Setup Definitions (verbatim-ish, 2026-07-05 chart review)

## Order block — what makes it REAL (AMD 06-10 example)
- OB = green candle **isolated from the volume/liquidity/price action before it** — "easier to look at and trust"
- Can be drawn from a super long wick, not just the whole candle body
- Then: CLEAR break, break candle "holding very strongly"
- Then: confirmation candle with very weak PA -> that's the entry
- Stop: bottom of the order block

## What KILLS an order block (QQQ 07-01 example)
- No strong-vs-weak candle contrast
- Too much consolidation around it

## A+ bar (MSFT 06-18)
- "Someone who knows very little should see it at first glance." Obviousness IS the grade.

## B&R stop refinement (QQQ 06-29)
- Stop can be tightened to a reference candle mid-trade for extra confluence
- HTF bias agreement justifies the trade

## OB + B&R confluence (rare, most significant)
- Strong displacement break above 5-min ORH (5-6 green candles)
- ONE red candle, then 3+ more green -> that red candle = the order block
- Price returns, retests OB with strong PA -> entry; stop = OB bottom
- Needs longer HTF breakout context

## 84% rule (Austin's simplified def — implemented 2026-07-05)
- Stop hit -> price reclaimed with same strong PA -> re-enter
- Hypothesis: hit ORIGINAL target 84% of time; "we just had our stop wrong the first time"

## OB vs B&R relationship
- OBs usually appear when there are NO significant levels for a B&R
- Both at once = strong confluence

## Trading params
- Window: 9:30–11:00 only (volume, options volume, volatility)
- Risk: $1,000/trade -> $2,000 win / $1,000 max loss
